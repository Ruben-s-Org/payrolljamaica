Here's how it works:

**As an employee:**
Every month, 2% of your gross wages goes to NHT. If you earn $80,000/month, that's $1,600/month, $19,200/year. For 7 years, that's over $134,000 in contributions (more with interest).

Hiring someone new in Jamaica? Here is the COMPLETE payroll onboarding guide — step by step.

I've seen so many Jamaica businesses get the hiring decision right and then fumble the payroll setup. This post covers everything.

---

**BEFORE THEIR FIRST DAY:**

✅ Confirm they have a TRN (Tax Registration Number)
Your employee's TRN is non-negotiable. Without it, you cannot correctly file PAYE for them. If they don't have one, direct them to the nearest TAJ office or etax.gov.jm to apply.

✅ Get their NIS number (or register them if new to formal employment)
The National Insurance Scheme requires every formally employed Jamaican to have an NIS number. New workers can register at NIS offices. This can take a few weeks, so start early.

✅ Register them with NHT
NHT requires employer registration for each employee. Their NHT contributions can only be tracked and credited correctly if they're in the NHT system. Register at nht.gov.jm.

---

**SETTING UP THEIR PAYROLL:**

✅ Enter salary/wage and pay frequency
Monthly, bi-weekly, or weekly — set this correctly from day one. PAYE is calculated based on annualized income, so the frequency affects the monthly deduction calculation.

✅ Apply correct tax code
Based on their TRN and employment status, confirm whether standard PAYE deductions apply. Check with TAJ if the employee has multiple income sources — their tax code may differ.

✅ Confirm all statutory deduction rates for 2026/2027:
→ PAYE: 25% above $1.5M threshold / 30% above $6M
→ NHT: 2% employee / 3% employer

**FIRST PAY PERIOD:**

✅ Run payroll — verify all deductions calculated correctly
✅ Issue pay slip showing all deductions line by line
✅ Remit all statutory contributions by the deadlines (NHT and PAYE by the 14th of following month, NIS per NIS schedule)
