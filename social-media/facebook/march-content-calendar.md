Employee salary: J$100,000

### Day 4 — Thu Mar 13 — EDUCATIONAL
```
📅 Jamaica Statutory Deduction Payment Deadlines

Mark these in your calendar:

PAYE → Due by the 14th of the following month
NHT → Due by the 14th of the following month
NIS → Due by the 14th of the following month
Education Tax → Due by the 14th of the following month

Simple rule: PAY BY THE 14TH.

Miss it? Here's what happens:
❌ Penalty: 50% of tax due
❌ Interest: 16.67% per annum on outstanding amount
❌ TAJ can pursue directors personally for company debts

These penalties add up FAST. One month late = serious money.

Don't let this happen to your business. Set up automatic reminders or better yet — use payroll software that tracks it for you.

### Day 5 — Fri Mar 14 — TIP OF THE WEEK
```
💡 Friday Payroll Tip for Jamaican Business Owners:

Did you know that payslips are LEGALLY required in Jamaica?

Under the Employment (Termination and Redundancy Payments) Act, you must give employees a written statement of their pay.

Your payslip must include:
✅ Employee name and details
✅ Pay period
✅ Gross pay
✅ Each deduction itemized (PAYE, NHT, NIS, Education Tax)
✅ Net pay

No payslip = potential compliance issue if an employee complains to the Labour Ministry.

Our system generates proper Jamaican payslips automatically.
