🏠 What Jamaican Employers Need to Know About NHT Contributions in 2026

NHT (National Housing Trust) contributions are mandatory for all employed Jamaicans — and the employer pays more than the employee.

Here's the breakdown:
• Employee contribution: 2% of gross salary
• Employer contribution: 3% of gross salary
• Total: 5% per employee per pay period

Who must contribute?
✅ All employed persons aged 18–65
✅ Both Jamaican and non-Jamaican employees working in Jamaica
❌ Self-employed persons contribute separately

Payment deadline: 14th of the following month

Late payment = 20% surcharge. Don't be late.

🛡️ Jamaica NIS Contributions — The Complete 2026 Guide for Employers

NIS (National Insurance Scheme) contributions are mandatory — and many employers don't realize they're paying MORE than they should (or less).

Current rates (2025/2026):
• Employee: 3% of insurable earnings
• Employer: 3% of insurable earnings
• Maximum insurable earnings: J$5,000,000/year
• Weekly cap: ~J$96,154

Key rules:
✅ Both employee AND employer pay 3% each
✅ There IS a ceiling — once you hit the annual cap, no more deductions
✅ Self-employed persons pay 6% (both portions)
✅ Must be paid to NIS by 14th of following month

Missing NIS payments = benefits denied to your employees + employer penalties
