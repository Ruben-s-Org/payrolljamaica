For most people, it is just the last day of the month. For Jamaican business owners, it is the end of the fiscal year — and if your payroll is not reconciled, TAJ will find out.

I have spoken with hundreds of Jamaican SME owners over the years. The payroll stress that builds up in March is predictable, preventable, and almost always caused by the same six things being out of order.

This article gives you the exact checklist to audit your payroll before the deadline. Work through it this week, not next week.

## Why March 31 Actually Matters

Jamaica's fiscal year runs April 1 to March 31. This date matters because:

**TAJ cross-references your annual P45/P60 submissions against monthly remittance records.** If what you reported monthly does not match what your annual return shows — that discrepancy triggers a review. Reviews become audits. Audits become penalties.

The penalty for late or incorrect statutory payments is not a gentle reminder. It is **50% of the outstanding amount**, plus **16.67% annual interest** on any balance. On a J$200,000 underpayment, you are looking at a J$100,000 penalty before interest. That is a real number that hits real cash flow.

The goal of this checklist is not to scare you — it is to give you 20 days to fix anything that is off, before TAJ's deadline makes that conversation much more expensive.

## The Six Statutory Obligations: Your Audit Checklist

Work through these in order. If any answer is "I'm not sure" — treat it as "no" and check your records.

---

### 1. PAYE — Pay As You Earn

**What it is:** Income tax deducted from employee salaries and remitted to TAJ by the 14th of each following month.

**Current thresholds (2025/26 tax year):**
- Annual income tax threshold: **J$1,500,096** (first J$1,500,096 is tax-free)
- Above threshold to J$6,000,000: **25% tax rate**
- Above J$6,000,000: **30% tax rate**

**Your audit questions:**
- Are you using the correct 2025/26 threshold — not last year's number?
- Did you remit PAYE by the 14th of every month from April 2025 through February 2026?
- Do your monthly TAJ receipts show all 11 remittances?

**Common error:** Many businesses still have the 2024/25 threshold in their spreadsheets. If you have not updated your PAYE calculation formula since April 2025, you have likely been under-deducting all year.

---

### 2. NHT — National Housing Trust

**What it is:** Mandatory contributions to the National Housing Trust. Both employer and employee pay.

**Current rates:**
- Employer: **3% of gross emoluments** (all employees, no ceiling)
- Employee: **2% of gross emoluments** (all employees, no ceiling)

**Your audit questions:**
- Are you paying 3% (employer share) in addition to the 2% deducted from employees?
- Is NHT calculated on gross emoluments — not net, not statutory income?
- Did you submit NHT contributions for every employee every month?

**Common error:** Confusing NHT's gross emoluments base with NIS's insurable wage ceiling. NHT has no upper limit — it applies to the full gross salary regardless of how high it goes.

---

### 3. NIS — National Insurance Scheme

**What it is:** National Insurance contributions providing pension, sickness, and maternity benefits.

**Current rates:**
- Employer: **3% of insurable wage** (up to the weekly insurable wage ceiling)
- Employee: **3% of insurable wage** (same ceiling)
- Weekly insurable wage ceiling: **J$5,000/week** (confirm current ceiling on NIS.gov.jm)

**Your audit questions:**
- Are you applying the ceiling correctly — not deducting NIS on earnings above the ceiling?
- Are both employer and employee contributions showing on payslips?
- Did you file monthly NIS returns and pay by the 14th?

**Common error:** Ignoring the insurable wage ceiling and calculating NIS on full gross salary. This results in over-deductions from employees — a potential legal liability, not just a compliance issue.

---

### 4. Education Tax

**What it is:** Tax to fund education in Jamaica. Charged on statutory income (not gross emoluments).

**Current rates:**
- Employer: **3.5% of statutory income**
- Employee: **2.25% of statutory income**

**Your audit questions:**
- Are you calculating Education Tax on **statutory income** — not gross salary?
- Statutory income = gross income minus approved pension contributions. Have you accounted for this correctly?
- Did you remit Education Tax with your monthly PAYE package?

**Common error:** Many businesses calculate Education Tax on gross salary rather than statutory income. The difference compounds across a year and a workforce.

---

### 5. Heart Trust / NSTA Levy

**What it is:** Training levy paid by employers to support workforce development.

**Current rate:**
- Employer only: **3% of total emoluments paid to employees**
- No employee deduction

**Your audit questions:**
- Are you paying 3% on total payroll — not just on individual salaries?
- Is this separated correctly on your payroll reports?

**Common error:** Forgetting Heart Trust entirely when calculating true cost of an employee. It is employer-only, easy to overlook, and mandatory.

---

### 6. Payslip Compliance

**What it is:** Employees in Jamaica are entitled to written payslips showing gross pay, all deductions, and net pay.

**Your audit questions:**
- Do you issue payslips every pay period (weekly, fortnightly, or monthly)?
- Does each payslip clearly show: gross pay, PAYE, NHT, NIS, Education Tax, other deductions, and net pay?
- Do you keep copies of payslips in employee records?

**Common error:** Processing payroll correctly but not issuing proper payslips. This leaves employers exposed in any employment dispute.

## The Three Warning Signs You Are Off Track

**1. You are using a spreadsheet you built 2+ years ago.**
Tax thresholds change every April 1. If you have not updated your PAYE formula since 2024, you have been calculating wrong for 11 months.

**2. You missed a 14th deadline at any point this year.**
Late payments attract 50% penalties automatically. Paying it now with interest is better than TAJ finding it later.

**3. You added staff mid-year and did not update your remittances.**
New hires must be on your NHT/NIS registers from their first pay period. A hire from August 2025 with no statutory record is a gap TAJ will flag.
