**Tweet 2:**
```
1/ EMPLOYEE DEDUCTIONS (what you take out of their gross pay each month):

• NIS → 3% of gross
• NHT → 2% of gross
• Education Tax → 2.25% of gross
• PAYE → 25% on annualised income above J$1,500,096/yr

These come OFF before the employee sees a dollar.
```

**Tweet 3:**
```
2/ EMPLOYER CONTRIBUTIONS (your cost ON TOP of the salary):

• NIS → 3% of gross
• NHT → 3% of gross
• Education Tax → 3.5% of gross
• HEART/NSTA Trust → 3% of gross

Hire someone at J$100k/month? Your ACTUAL monthly cost is J$109,500+
```

**Tweet 4:**
```
3/ THE PAYE THRESHOLDS (2025/26 tax year):

• 0% on income up to J$1,500,096/year (J$125,008/month)
• 25% on income J$1.5M–J$6M/year
• 30% on income above J$6M/year

Important: these are ANNUALISED. Monthly pay needs to be grossed up before applying.
Many spreadsheets get this wrong.
```

**Tweet 5:**
```
4/ THE STATUTORY RETURNS YOU MUST FILE:

📋 S01 → TAJ, every month (PAYE/NIS/Ed Tax/NHT combined)
📋 NHT Annual Return → directly to NHT, year-end
📋 P6 form → give to EVERY employee for their personal tax
📋 NIS Annual Certificate → NIS records reconciled

Miss any of these = penalty notices.
```

**Tweet 6:**
```
5/ NIS CEILING (the one most people forget):

NIS contributions stop once an employee's gross pay hits J$5,000,000/year.

After the ceiling → no more NIS deductions for that employee that tax year.

If you're deducting NIS on earnings above J$5M — you're over-deducting. That's TAJ's problem to fix.
```
