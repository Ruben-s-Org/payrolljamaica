### Thread 1: "How Jamaica PAYE Works in 2026 🧵"
```
1/ How does Jamaica PAYE actually work? A thread for every business owner in Jamaica 🇯🇲

2/ PAYE = Pay As You Earn. It's income tax deducted directly from your employee's salary every pay period.

3/ The threshold: First J$1,500,096 of annual income is TAX FREE.
Above that? 25% up to J$6M, then 30%.

4/ Your job as employer: Deduct the right amount from salary, pay it to TAJ by the 14th of the following month.

5/ Miss the deadline? 50% penalty + 16.67% annual interest on outstanding. TAJ is serious about this.

### Thread 2: "Everything You Need to Know About NHT 🧵"
```
1/ NHT contributions confuse a lot of Jamaican employers. Let me clear it up. 🧵

2/ NHT = National Housing Trust. Both you AND your employee contribute every pay period.

3/ Rates:
Employee: 2% of gross salary
Employer: 3% of gross salary
Total: 5% per employee

4/ No ceiling on NHT (unlike NIS). You pay 5% on the full salary, no matter how high.

5/ Payment: Due 14th of following month to NHT office.
Miss it? Surcharge of 20% of outstanding amount.

6/ The contribution builds your employee's NHT benefit — they can use it to get a home loan. It matters.

7/ Calculate NHT automatically: https://payrolljamaica.com/calculator
