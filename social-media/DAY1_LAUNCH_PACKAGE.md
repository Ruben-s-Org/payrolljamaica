NHT contributions are the #1 payroll error I see in Jamaican SME audits.

Not because business owners are careless. Because the rules have more nuance than most HR manuals explain.

Let me clear up the top 3 NHT mistakes:

Mistake 1: Not including allowances in the contribution base
NHT is calculated on "gross emoluments" — which includes housing allowances, car allowances, and other regular payments. Many employers calculate only on base salary.

Mistake 2: Applying NHT to casual workers incorrectly
Workers employed for less than 8 hours per week are generally exempt. If you're classifying part-time staff incorrectly, you may be under-contributing.

Mistake 3: Late remittance (even by one day)
NHT charges a 20% penalty on late contributions. A J$50,000 contribution that's 30 days late costs you J$10,000 in penalties. That's real money.

All three of these are caught automatically by our calculator before you submit.
