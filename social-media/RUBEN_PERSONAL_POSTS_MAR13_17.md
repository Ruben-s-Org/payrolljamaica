**Platform:** LinkedIn Personal
**Time:** 9:00 AM – 10:00 AM  
**Theme:** Education / NHT focus

```
Quick Jamaica payroll fact that trips up a lot of employers:

NIS has a ceiling. PAYE has a threshold. NHT does not.

What this means:

• NIS: Employee contribution stops once annual earnings hit J$5,000,000
  → After that point, no more NIS deduction for the year
  → Many employers miss this and keep deducting — that's an error

• PAYE: Only applies to income above J$1,500,096/year (J$125,008/month)
  → Below threshold: NO PAYE owed
  → Above threshold: 25% on the excess, 30% above J$6M

• NHT: No ceiling. 2% every single pay period, all year, no exception.

These three rules alone cause the majority of Jamaican payroll errors.
```

```
The Jamaica SO1 Return. The most important document most Jamaican business owners have never seen.

What is it?

The SO1 is your monthly employer statutory declaration — filed with the Tax Administration Jamaica (TAJ).

It reconciles:
• Total wages paid
• PAYE deducted
• NIS (employee + employer)
• NHT (employee + employer)
• Education Tax

Every employer with at least one employee must file this monthly. It's due by the 14th of the following month.

What happens if you file late or incorrectly?
→ Automatic penalty
→ Interest on underpayments
→ TAJ correspondence that takes months to resolve
→ In serious cases: court summons

With 14 days to March 31, you'll need to file the March SO1 by April 14. But your ENTIRE year's records need to reconcile first.
```
