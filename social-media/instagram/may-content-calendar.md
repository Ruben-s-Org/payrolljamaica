**SLIDE 2:** "WHAT IS NHT?"
National Housing Trust. Established 1976. One mission: help Jamaican workers own homes.
Every employed Jamaican contributes 2% of their wages every month. Your employer adds 3%.

**SLIDE 3:** "THE 7-YEAR REFUND"
After 7 years of NHT contributions, you are eligible for a FULL REFUND of your employee contributions — with interest. This is a real payment to you. Thousands of Jamaicans qualify right now and don't know it. Check your eligibility: nht.gov.jm

**SLIDE 4:** "THE NHT MORTGAGE BENEFIT"
NHT offers home loans at rates as low as 0% to qualifying contributors. Commercial bank mortgage rates in Jamaica: 8–10%. On a $15 million mortgage, the difference over 30 years is extraordinary. Your NHT contributions are building toward this.

**SLIDE 5:** "WHO QUALIFIES?"
To access NHT mortgage benefits: at least 7 years of contributions, minimum contribution amount met, property must qualify. Check nht.gov.jm for full eligibility criteria and apply — the process is available online.

**SLIDE 6:** "WHY YOUR EMPLOYER'S REMITTANCE MATTERS"
When you apply for your NHT mortgage or refund, NHT checks your contribution history against your employer's remittance records. If your employer deducted NHT but didn't remit it — your application stalls. Correct, on-time employer remittances protect your benefit.

**SLIDE 7:** "FOR EMPLOYERS — YOUR NHT RESPONSIBILITY"
→ Calculate NHT on gross wages (2% employee / 3% employer)
→ Remit to NHT employer portal by the 14th of each month
→ Ensure every employee is registered in the NHT system
→ Keep remittance records — employees can request contribution statements

**SLIDE 2:** "THE LAW"
Jamaica Holidays (Public General) Act governs all gazetted public holidays. Labour Day is a gazetted public holiday. The rules are mandatory — no opting out.

**SLIDE 3:** "EMPLOYEES NOT WORKING — What They're Owed"
✓ One full day's pay at their regular daily rate
✓ Condition: Must have worked the last scheduled working day before the holiday (typically Friday, May 22)
✓ If absent without authorization on May 22: employer can withhold holiday pay (must be documented)

TWO options (employer's choice):
Option A: 2x their regular daily rate for hours worked on Labour Day
Option B: Regular pay for the day + a full compensatory day off at a mutually agreed date
Neither option can pay less than double. This is non-negotiable.

Running Labour Day hours at regular rates and hoping employees don't notice. They will notice. And a Ministry of Labour claim for underpaid holiday pay is time-consuming, costs legal fees, includes interest on the underpayment, and always ends up costing more than the original correct payment.
