**SLIDE 2:** Q: "What is the income tax threshold in Jamaica?"
A: The personal income tax threshold is $1,500,096 annually (verify against latest TAJ announcement for 2026). Employees earning below this pay zero income tax. Above it: 25% up to $6M, then 30%.

**SLIDE 3:** Q: "Do part-time employees get PAYE deducted?"
A: Yes — if their annual equivalent earnings exceed the tax threshold, PAYE applies. For part-timers, annualize their income to determine if deductions apply.

**SLIDE 4:** Q: "My employee says they don't want NHT deducted. Can I stop it?"
A: No. NHT contributions are mandatory by law for all employed Jamaicans. The employer (3%) and employee (2%) contributions cannot be waived or opted out of. They're also not losing money — those contributions build toward housing benefits and refunds.

**SLIDE 5:** Q: "What happens if I pay PAYE late to TAJ?"
A: TAJ charges interest on late remittances. Persistent late payment can result in penalties, interest compounding, and in serious cases, enforcement action. The deadline is the 14th of the month following payroll.

**SLIDE 6:** Q: "Does my domestic helper count as an employee I need to do payroll for?"
A: If you're paying a domestic worker (helper, gardener, driver) a regular wage, technically statutory deductions apply. In practice, many small household employers don't comply. But if you're running a formal business, all employees require proper payroll.

**SLIDE 7:** Q: "What is the statutory income deduction?"
A: Under PAYE rules, 1/3 of gross income (up to $500,000) is excluded from taxable income. This deduction reduces the PAYE liability for all employees. If your payroll isn't applying this, you're over-deducting.

**SLIDE 8:** Q: "How do I get a TRN for a new employee?"
A: The employee applies at Tax Administration Jamaica (TAJ) — either in person at a TAJ office or online. As an employer, you cannot apply on their behalf but you should confirm they have a TRN before their first payroll.

**SLIDE 9:** Q: "Can I run payroll myself or do I need an accountant?"
A: With the right software, yes — you can run payroll yourself. PayrollJamaica is designed for business owners with no payroll expertise. Built-in Jamaica tax rules mean you don't need to know the calculations — the software handles them.
