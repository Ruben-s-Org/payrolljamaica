## COMMENT RESPONSE TEMPLATES

### Category 1 — Genuine Questions (High Priority)

**"How does PAYE work in Jamaica?"**
> Great question! PAYE in Jamaica is taxed progressively: 0% on the first J$1.5M of annual income, then 25% up to J$6M, and 30% above J$6M. Your employer is responsible for calculating and remitting monthly. Want the exact breakdown for your salary? Our free calculator does it instantly → payrolljamaica.com/calculator 🇯🇲

**"What is the NHT rate for 2026?"**
> NHT contributions in Jamaica: Employees contribute 2% of gross pay. Employers contribute 3%. No ceiling on NHT (unlike NIS). Both go to the National Housing Trust monthly. Our payroll calculator includes NHT auto-calculation → payrolljamaica.com/calculator

**"When is the SO1 deadline?"**
> March 31 — that's the Jamaica fiscal year-end. The SO1 Annual Return covers April 2025 to March 2026 and must be submitted to TAJ. With 19 days left, now's a good time to start reconciling. PayrollJamaica exports SO1-ready data automatically → payrolljamaica.com

**"How many employees trigger payroll requirements?"**
> In Jamaica, the obligation to register as an employer and deduct PAYE/NHT/NIS starts with your FIRST employee. There's no threshold — if you pay anyone, you're responsible for statutory deductions from day one. Happy to walk you through the setup → payrolljamaica.com

**"What's the difference between NHT and NIS?"**
> Good one to clarify! NIS (National Insurance Scheme) covers employee social security — disability, retirement, sickness. Employer pays 3%, employee pays 3% (on capped earnings of ~J$1.5M/year). NHT (National Housing Trust) funds housing loans — employer pays 3%, employee pays 2%, NO ceiling. Both are compulsory. Both have March 31 annual return deadlines 👆

**"Can I run payroll myself without software?"**
> Absolutely — it's done with spreadsheets all the time. The challenge is keeping up with annual rate changes (TAJ adjusts thresholds each April), avoiding calculation errors across NHT/NIS/PAYE/EdTax, and generating accurate SO1 and P6 data at year-end. Many businesses start manual and switch when the complexity grows. We built PayrollJamaica specifically for this → payrolljamaica.com/calculator to see how the math works.
