### Here's what you need to do before March 31:

**✅ 1. Reconcile your PAYE deductions**
The income tax threshold for 2025/2026 is **J$1,500,096 annually** (J$125,008/month). Confirm that every employee earning above this threshold had the correct 25% PAYE withheld each pay period. Over- or under-deduction must be corrected before year-end.

**✅ 2. Verify NHT contributions**
The National Housing Trust requires **3% from the employee and 3% from the employer**, applied to gross wages. Check that both sides have been remitted monthly. Missed contributions carry interest penalties.

**✅ 3. Confirm NIS contributions**
National Insurance Scheme contributions are **3% employee, 3% employer**, capped at the prescribed ceiling. These must be reconciled across every pay period and any shortfalls paid before filing.

**✅ 4. Education Tax — di one people forget**
This one slips through the cracks every year. Education Tax is **2.25% from the employee** and **3.5% from the employer**, applied to taxable income (after NIS deduction). If your payroll software isn't calculating this correctly, you may already owe arrears.

**✅ 5. Prepare your SO1 annual return documents**
Your SO1 is filed with the Tax Administration Jamaica (TAJ). It summarises every employee's earnings, deductions, and PAYE withheld for the full year. Late or incorrect filing can cost up to **J$10,000 per employee**. Gather your payroll records now — don't leave this for the last week.
