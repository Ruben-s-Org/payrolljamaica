The most expensive payroll mistake in Jamaica:

Getting the deduction order wrong.

Here's the correct sequence every pay period:

Step 1 → Calculate GROSS pay
Step 2 → Deduct NIS (3% of gross) — this lowers the base for PAYE
Step 3 → Deduct NHT (2% of gross)
Step 4 → Deduct Education Tax (2.25% of gross)
Step 5 → Apply PAYE threshold: J$1,500,096 annual / J$125,008 monthly
Step 6 → PAYE = 25% on taxable income above threshold

Why does order matter?

Because NIS is deducted BEFORE the PAYE calculation. If you're running PAYE on full gross pay, you're overtaxing employees and understating your own deduction liability.
