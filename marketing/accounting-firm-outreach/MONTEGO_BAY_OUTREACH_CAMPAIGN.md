## 1. Why Montego Bay — Strategic Rationale

### MoBay is Jamaica's Second-Most Important Business Hub — and It's Underserved

Kingston gets all the attention. We've been running outreach there for weeks. Montego Bay is the strategic move that most software companies miss.

**The case for MoBay right now:**

| Factor | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **Second-largest city in Jamaica** | Significant SME economy — hotels, BPOs, retail chains, construction firms, transport, manufacturing |
| **Tourism capital of the island** | Jamaica's highest-volume hospitality corridor: Sandals Montego Bay, Half Moon, Secrets Wild Orchid, dozens of mid-size hotels, villas, and restaurants — all with complex payroll |
| **Less saturated than Kingston** | Accounting firms in MoBay receive far fewer pitches from Kingston-based SaaS companies. Lower noise = higher response rates |
| **Tourism payroll is notoriously complex** | Service charge allocation, tip pooling, seasonal staff (tourist season peaks Dec–April), daily workers, and multi-rate pay structures — exactly the pain points PayrollJamaica solves |
| **Fiscal year urgency** | Jamaica's fiscal year ends March 31. MoBay accountants are deep in year-end work. They are acutely aware of payroll compliance right now. This is the perfect moment to land in their inbox |
| **BPO sector growth** | Montego Bay is Jamaica's leading BPO hub (Teleperformance, Concentrix, etc.). The firms serving those companies handle large employee rosters and shifting pay structures — ideal PayrollJamaica users |
| **Airport access** | Sangster International Airport means MoBay is well-connected — once we have a partner there, in-person demos and relationship-building trips are feasible |

### Target Persona: The MoBay Accounting Firm Principal

- Typically a sole practitioner or 2-10 person firm
- Handles 15-60 SME clients (mix of hospitality, retail, professional services)
- Currently managing payroll manually or via spreadsheets for most clients
- Pain point: The March 31 year-end creates a crunch — they want solutions they can hand off
- Motivated by: recurring revenue, reduced errors on compliance, and tools they can white-label or recommend confidently

### Engagement Rules for Facebook Groups

1. **Do not post the same content in multiple groups on the same day.** Space posts 2-3 days apart.
2. **Comment first before posting.** Engage with 2-3 other posts in the group before dropping your own — this signals you're a real member, not a drive-by spammer.
3. **Respond to every comment within 2 hours** during business hours. Group algorithms reward engagement velocity.
4. **Do not drop a link without context.** Always frame the link as a resource, not a sales pitch.
5. **Follow up in DM** with anyone who comments positively — don't let warm leads go cold in the comments.

## Key Differentiators to Emphasize for MoBay Firms

1. **Service charge compliance:** PayrollJamaica handles the Tourism (Service Charge) Act requirements automatically — how the 10% charge is allocated, net of deductions, distributed to eligible staff.
2. **Seasonal staff management:** Start/end dates, different NIS contribution tiers, rehire logic for seasonal workers at resorts.
3. **Multi-rate pay:** Daily workers, hourly staff, and salaried employees on the same payroll — common in MoBay hotels.
4. **Tip pooling:** Custom logic for how gratuity is distributed across departments.
5. **NHT and NIS bulk filings:** One-click statutory filing for firms managing 10+ client payrolls.
