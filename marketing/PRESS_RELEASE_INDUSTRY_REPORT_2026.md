## PayrollJamaica Publishes Jamaica's First Dedicated Payroll Industry Report, Revealing 70% of SMEs at Risk of TAJ Penalties

### New research shows the majority of Jamaican businesses still rely on manual payroll methods that expose them to the 25% TAJ surcharge and rising compliance costs

**Kingston, Jamaica — March 2026** — PayrollJamaica, Jamaica's dedicated cloud payroll platform, today released the *Jamaica Payroll Industry Report 2026*, a comprehensive data-driven examination of payroll compliance practices, statutory obligations, and the true cost of payroll mismanagement among Jamaica's estimated 50,000 formally registered businesses.

The report, available free at www.payrolljamaica.com, is the most detailed analysis of SME payroll compliance to date in Jamaica and arrives with the March 31 annual returns deadline weeks away — the single most consequential date on the Jamaican payroll calendar.

---

### Key Findings

**1. Seven in ten Jamaican SMEs process payroll manually.**
An estimated 70% of small and medium-sized enterprises rely on spreadsheets, manual ledger books, or informal calculations to manage employee payroll — a practice that systematically produces errors in PAYE deductions, NHT remittances, and NIS calculations.

**2. The NHT ceiling error is widespread and expensive.**
One of the most common payroll mistakes among Jamaican businesses is applying an income ceiling to NHT contributions — when in fact the National Housing Trust Act requires contributions on 100% of gross salary with no ceiling. Businesses making this error are chronically under-remitting to NHT, accumulating a debt that attracts TAJ penalties and interest.

**3. A single TAJ penalty event can cost a small business JMD 300,000–800,000.**
When the 25% surcharge on underpaid tax, statutory interest, and accountant remediation fees are added together, a TAJ compliance review can cost a 10–20 person business more than JMD 300,000 — equivalent to years of payroll software subscription fees.

**4. Employer statutory costs add 9.5% to every dollar of gross payroll.**
Many Jamaican business owners are unaware that for every JMD 100 of gross salary paid, they owe an additional JMD 9.50 in employer-side NIS, NHT, and Education Tax contributions. A business with JMD 1,000,000/month in payroll carries JMD 95,000/month in employer statutory obligations on top.

**5. Automated payroll saves 6–10 hours per month for a 10-person team.**
Businesses transitioning from manual or spreadsheet payroll to a purpose-built platform eliminate the most time-intensive payroll tasks and reduce their statutory error rate dramatically — with direct impact on cash flow, compliance, and employee satisfaction.
