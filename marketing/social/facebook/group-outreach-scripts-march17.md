### Post A — Common PAYE mistake

**Topic:** Educational — most common PAYE mistake small businesses make

**Copy:**

The most common PAYE mistake I see small Jamaican businesses make: calculating PAYE on gross salary instead of statutory income.

Here's the difference. Before you calculate PAYE, you first need to subtract:
- NIS contributions (3% of gross, up to the NIS ceiling)
- The annual tax threshold (J$1,500,096)

PAYE is only calculated on what's left after those deductions. A lot of businesses skip the NIS deduction step and end up over-deducting from employees — which becomes a headache to correct, especially at year-end when you're filing the SO1.

With 19 days to March 31, if your numbers haven't been reconciled yet, now is the time to check. Anyone want me to walk through an example calculation?

Something a lot of employees don't know: if you've been contributing to NHT for at least 3 years and haven't used the benefit, you may be eligible to claim a refund of your contributions going back 7 years.

### Post C — SO1 filing steps

**Topic:** Practical walkthrough of SO1 filing

**Copy:**

If you haven't filed your SO1 before or it's been a few years, here's roughly what the process looks like:

1. Log into the TAJ e-Services portal (tajonline.gov.jm)
2. Select "Annual Return" under PAYE
3. Enter each employee's name, TRN, gross emoluments, and total PAYE deducted
4. The system reconciles against your monthly remittances
5. If there's a shortfall, you settle it before or at submission

The trickiest part is step 4 — making sure your monthly remittances actually match what you enter. If you've been calculating PAYE manually and any month was slightly off, that shows up here.

Deadline is March 31. Penalty for late filing is up to J$10,000 per employee listed.

If anyone wants to double-check their per-employee PAYE calculations before filing, payrolljamaica.com/calculator breaks it down for free.

### Post A — Technical PAYE calculation thread

**Topic:** PAYE calculation order of operations — common processing error

**Copy:**

For anyone processing payroll manually or auditing a client's books before the March 31 SO1 deadline — a quick note on calculation sequence that I see mishandled regularly.

The correct order for monthly PAYE:

1. Start with gross emoluments
2. Deduct NIS (3% of gross, employer ceiling is J$5M annually so most employees don't hit it)
3. Deduct the monthly tax threshold: J$125,008
4. What remains is statutory income — PAYE is 25% of this amount

If statutory income is zero or negative, PAYE is zero for that month.

The error I see most often: deducting the threshold before NIS, or forgetting the threshold entirely and applying 25% to gross minus NIS. Both produce wrong numbers. The first overstates the threshold benefit, the second understates it.

Anyone doing year-end reconciliation — worth verifying the calculation method used for each month, not just the totals.

#PayrollJamaica #PAYE

### Post B — SO1 reconciliation tips

**Topic:** Practical advice for HR professionals reconciling before SO1 filing

**Copy:**

With SO1 deadline three weeks out, here are the reconciliation steps I'd prioritize for any client or employer:

1. Pull all 12 monthly P6/remittance receipts from TAJ
2. Reconcile total PAYE remitted vs. total PAYE per individual payslips
3. Identify any months where an employee's tax situation changed (new hire, termination, salary change, exempt status)
4. Check NIS contributions separately — the 3% employee, 3% employer structure is straightforward, but the J$5M annual ceiling catches payroll for higher earners
5. Reconcile NHT separately for the NHT annual return (separate portal, same March 31 deadline)

If there are variances, it's much better to identify and address them before filing than to have TAJ flag them afterward.

The penalty for late SO1 is up to J$10,000 per employee. For a company with 50 employees that files late, that exposure is J$500,000.

Happy to discuss any specific scenarios here.

### Post B — NIS ceiling issue for high-earning employees

**Topic:** NIS ceiling — the compliance issue that trips up accountants and payroll staff

**Copy:**

Something worth double-checking for clients with higher-earning employees: the NIS annual ceiling.

NIS contributions (both employee and employer sides) are capped at a J$5,000,000 annual gross. For employees earning above J$416,667/month gross, contributions stop once the cumulative annual gross hits J$5M.

In practice this means: if you have an employee earning J$600,000/month, NIS is only calculated on the first J$5M of their annual earnings (~8.3 months), not the full year. After that, their NIS contribution for the year is done.

A lot of payroll setups I've seen either continue deducting NIS all year (overdeduction) or don't track the cumulative threshold at all.

With year-end reconciliation due March 31, this is one worth specifically checking for any employee on the higher end of the salary range.
