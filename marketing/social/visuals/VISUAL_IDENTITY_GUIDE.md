# PayrollJamaica — Social Media Visual Identity Guide

## Brand Philosophy

PayrollJamaica sits at the intersection of local pride and modern professionalism. The visual identity should communicate: **"We understand Jamaica. We speak your language. And we're serious about your business."**

Avoid two failure modes:
- **Too generic**: looks like any SaaS company anywhere. No soul.
- **Too "Jamaica-themed"**: reggae flags, tourist vibes, clichéd imagery. Undermines trust.

The goal is a brand that a Kingston CFO and a Montego Bay SME owner both feel was built for them.

## Brand Colors

### Primary Palette

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|------|-----|-------|
| Primary | Deep Forest | `#1A3A2A` | Headers, CTAs, primary backgrounds |
| Accent | Solar Gold | `#F2A900` | Highlights, key numbers, underlines |
| Base | Slate White | `#F7F8F6` | Card backgrounds, light sections |
| Text | Near Black | `#111111` | Body copy, headings |

### Secondary Palette

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|------|-----|-------|
| Support | Muted Sage | `#4A7C59` | Secondary elements, tags, dividers |
| Warm | Amber Light | `#FFF3CC` | Highlight backgrounds, callout boxes |
| Cool | Steel Blue | `#2D6A8F` | Charts, data visualization, links |
| Neutral | Ash | `#8A8A8A` | Captions, secondary text, borders |

### Color Logic

**Inspiration, not imitation**: The Jamaica flag (black, gold, green) is the emotional root, but evolved for a tech context:
- Black → Deep Forest (warmer, less cold, signals local roots)
- Gold → Solar Gold (kept almost pure — confident, premium)
- Green → Muted Sage and Deep Forest (sophisticated, not neon)

**Never use**: Raw red, harsh neon, or the raw Jamaican flag colors directly. They read as "flag" not "brand."

**Color pairings that work**:
- Deep Forest + Solar Gold — authority, premium
- Slate White + Steel Blue — clean data, trust
- Amber Light + Near Black — warm, approachable

## Typography

### Primary Typefaces (Google Fonts — free in Canva)

**Headings: Sora (Bold, ExtraBold)**
- Use for: Post headlines, carousel slide titles, stat numbers
- Why: Modern geometric, confident, excellent at large sizes
- Sizes: 36–72pt for social, 48–96pt for Stories/Reels

**Body/Subheading: DM Sans (Regular, Medium, SemiBold)**
- Use for: Body copy, captions, slide body text, form labels
- Why: Humanist, readable at small sizes, friendly without being informal
- Sizes: 14–24pt for body, 24–32pt for subheadings

**Accent (use sparingly): DM Mono**
- Use for: Numbers, percentages, tax rates, code/data callouts
- Why: Signals precision and technical accuracy
- Example: "NIS: **3%**" in DM Mono feels authoritative

### Type Rules

- **Hierarchy is law**: One dominant element per slide. Never compete for attention.
- **Line length**: Max 8–10 words per headline on social. No walls of text.
- **Contrast minimum**: White text on Dark Forest passes WCAG AA. Always test.
- **Tracking**: Headlines at -10 to -20 (slightly tighter). Body at 0.
- **Never use**: Comic Sans, Papyrus, Trajan, or any handwritten font. No script fonts.

## Logo Usage Guidelines

### Social Profile Images

- Use the logomark only (icon/symbol, not full wordmark) for profile pictures
- Minimum size: 200×200px. Optimal: 400×400px
- Background: Deep Forest (`#1A3A2A`) or Slate White (`#F7F8F6`)
- Clear space: equal to the height of the "P" glyph on all four sides
- Never crop the logo, apply drop shadows, or place on busy imagery

### In-Post Logo Use

- Bottom-right or bottom-left corner of posts — not centered, not competing with content
- Size: approximately 15–20% of post width
- Version: horizontal wordmark preferred on landscape; icon on portrait/Stories
- Watermark opacity for templates: 80% — visible but not dominant

### What Not to Do with the Logo

- Do not place on gradients with insufficient contrast
- Do not stretch, rotate, or distort
- Do not use the wordmark in Solar Gold on white — insufficient contrast
- Do not recreate the logo in text — always use the approved file

## Image Style

### Photography

**What to use:**
- Real Jamaican business environments: office settings in Kingston, Montego Bay, New Kingston financial district
- Diverse professionals: accountants, HR managers, business owners — not stock-generic, not tourist-facing
- Overhead "work in progress" shots: laptop + notebook + coffee on a clean desk
- Close-up hands on keyboard or reviewing documents
- Natural light preferred. Warm, golden-hour tones align with the brand palette

**What to avoid:**
- Generic international stock (white background, suits that scream "iStock 2015")
- Sunset over the beach, jerk chicken, Usain Bolt references — this is business software
- Overly staged "team high-five" corporate clichés
- Dark, moody, or desaturated tones — the brand is warm and confident

**Color treatment for photos:**
- Apply a subtle Deep Forest overlay at 15–20% opacity to unify photography with brand palette
- Alternatively, convert to a two-tone (duotone) using Deep Forest + Solar Gold for stylized carousel covers

### Illustration / Icons

- Use flat, geometric icon style. Phosphor Icons or Streamline match the brand tone
- Icon color: Deep Forest, Muted Sage, or Steel Blue — never full black
- Illustrations (if used): clean line illustrations, not cartoon/mascot style
- Data visualizations: bar charts, donut charts in brand palette — Steel Blue as primary, Solar Gold as accent

### Graphic Elements

- Thin horizontal rule in Solar Gold used as a divider — signature brand element
- Dot grid or subtle diagonal line texture at very low opacity as background texture
- Rounded corners on all cards: 12–16px radius (soft but structured)
- No drop shadows heavier than `0 4px 12px rgba(0,0,0,0.08)`

## Do's and Don'ts

### DO

- Lead with the number or stat — "4 hours saved" is more powerful than "efficiency gains"
- Use white space aggressively. Empty space communicates confidence.
- Keep every post anchored to a Jamaica-specific detail (NHT, PAYE, statutory rates, NIS)
- Use the Solar Gold underline or highlight to draw attention to the single most important word
- Match image warmth to the palette — edit photos to feel cohesive
- Show real product UI screenshots in a clean device frame (MacBook or iPhone mockup)
- Add the URL (`payrolljamaica.com`) as a small text element on every post

### DON'T

- Don't use more than 3 fonts in a single post
- Don't combine more than 3 colors in a single post (stick to 2 + neutral)
- Don't post blurry screenshots — use actual screen recordings or clean exports
- Don't use all caps for body text (only for short labels/tags)
- Don't mix illustration styles in the same post or carousel
- Don't use gradients that span more than 2 colors — subtle, directional gradients only
- Don't put the logo in a color that wasn't specified in this guide
- Don't use clipart, emoji-heavy graphics, or anything that looks like a WhatsApp forward

## Brand Voice in Visuals

Every visual decision should ask: **"Does this look like it belongs to a company a Jamaican CFO would trust with their payroll?"**

The answer should always be yes.

Visual tone benchmarks:
- **Professional** (not stuffy) — like a sharp Kingston law firm, not a government form
- **Local** (not tourist) — like The Gleaner's business section, not Visit Jamaica
- **Modern** (not generic) — like Stripe or Gusto, but rooted in JA reality
- **Warm** (not cold) — approachable expertise, not intimidating tech
