# PayrollJamaica — Content Repurposing Matrix

Work smarter, not harder. Every piece of content should generate at least 8 social assets before it's retired. This matrix maps exactly how to do it.

---

## Core Principle

**One idea, one batch session, one week of content.**

The goal is not to repeat yourself. Each asset reframes the same idea for a different context, attention span, or platform. A LinkedIn reader and an Instagram scroller need different things — but both can come from the same source material.

---

## 1. Blog Post → 3 Platforms × 3 Formats (9 assets)

**Source example:** "How to Calculate PAYE in Jamaica: A 2026 Guide"

### Platform: LinkedIn

| Format | Asset Description | Execution |
|--------|------------------|-----------|
| Long-form post | Adapted blog intro + 5 key takeaways + link | Copy the hook sentence. Add 4 bullet points. End with "Full breakdown at the link." ~200 words. |
| Carousel (5 slides) | One key concept per slide, visual summary | Use Template 01. Pull the 5 clearest points from the blog. Each slide = one insight. |
| Quote card | Pull the most quotable line from the blog | Use Template 08. One sentence that stands alone. Post as a standalone graphic. |

### Platform: Instagram

| Format | Asset Description | Execution |
|--------|------------------|-----------|
| Step-by-step carousel | Turn the "how to" section into slides | Use Template 02. Blog's numbered steps become carousel slides. |
| Stat card | Pull the most surprising number from the blog | Use Template 03. E.g., "J$1.7M — what you can earn tax-free in 2026" |
| Story series | 3–5 Stories that tease the blog with swipe-up | One stat/hook per Story. Poll on the last one: "Did you know this?" |

### Platform: Facebook

| Format | Asset Description | Execution |
|--------|------------------|-----------|
| Long post | Full blog summary (~300 words) with link | Facebook audiences read longer posts. Paste a condensed version, add link. |
| Deadline reminder card | If blog covers dates, make a deadline card | Use Template 05. Extract the relevant deadline and post it standalone. |
| "Did you know?" card | One fact from the blog as a shareable image | Use Template 10. Built for screenshots and WhatsApp-forward behavior. |

**Production time:** 1 blog post → 9 assets in ~3 hours with the Canva templates pre-built.

## 2. Calculator Result → Shareable Graphic (4 assets)

**Source:** A user runs payroll for a JMD 100,000/month employee and sees the breakdown.

**Goal:** Make the output feel useful and shareable, not just functional.

### Asset A — Stat Card (Instagram/Facebook)
- Pull the "net take-home" result: e.g., "On a J$100K salary, your employee takes home J$87,240"
- Use Template 03. Solar Gold number, white background, one line of context.
- Caption: "See what YOUR payroll breakdown looks like → payrolljamaica.com/calculator"

### Asset B — Deduction Breakdown Infographic
- Visualize the full breakdown: Gross → NIS → NHT → Ed Tax → PAYE → Net
- Use a horizontal bar or stacked flow chart in brand colors
- Each deduction segment labeled with % and JMD amount
- Text: "This is where your employee's J$100K goes every month"

### Asset C — Comparison Card
- Left side: "Old way (manual Excel)" — time + error risk
- Right side: "PayrollJamaica calculator" — instant, accurate
- Format: Template 06 (LinkedIn professional tip layout)
- This works particularly well for LinkedIn decision-makers

### Asset D — Story Poll
- Use Template 07
- Question: "Did you know how much your employer pays on top of your salary?"
- Poll: Yes / No — I'm surprised
- Follow-up Story: "Here's the full breakdown → [link in bio]"

## 3. Customer Testimonial → 4 Formats

**Source:** A customer says: "PayrollJamaica saved me 4 hours every payroll run. I used to dread the 14th. Now I actually look forward to it."

### Format A — Quote Card (Instagram/Facebook)
- Use Template 08 (Quote Card)
- Pull: "I used to dread the 14th. Now I actually look forward to it."
- Attribution: "— SME owner, Kingston"
- Add their photo (with permission) or use a silhouette icon
- This is the highest-performing format for testimonials

### Format B — LinkedIn Professional Testimonial Post
- Write a 3-paragraph post:
  1. Para 1: Set the scene — "Running payroll for a growing team in Jamaica is not simple."
  2. Para 2: Quote — [full testimonial]
  3. Para 3: CTA — "We built PayrollJamaica specifically for businesses like this."
- No image needed — the text is the content
- Tag the customer if they consent

### Format C — Instagram Carousel (Problem → Solution → Proof)
- Slide 1: The problem — "Do you dread payroll day?"
- Slide 2: Why it's hard — manual, error-prone, penalties if wrong
- Slide 3: The customer's before state
- Slide 4: Quote from testimonial
- Slide 5: CTA — "Join them"

### Format D — Reel / Short Video (if customer is willing)
- 30-second talking-head clip: "Before PayrollJamaica I did X. Now I do Y."
- Use Reel cover Template 04
- Add text overlays: the key stat ("4 hours saved") in Solar Gold
- Close with URL on screen

**If customer can't appear on camera:** Use the quote over B-roll of someone at a laptop, calendar, or payslip. Text on screen carries the message.

## 4. Jamaica News / Regulation Change → Rapid Response Content Plan

**Trigger example:** "Government announces NIS rate increase from 3% to 4% effective July 1"

**Goal:** Be the first trusted voice explaining what this means for Jamaican businesses.

**Timeline and assets:**

### Hour 1 — Social First (post while it's hot)
- **Twitter/X + Facebook text post:** One paragraph. Plain text. "BREAKING: Jamaica NIS rate changing. Here's what it means for your payroll." Link to TAJ announcement.
- **Instagram Story:** Use a Story template with "NEW RATE ALERT" badge. Quick stats. Poll: "Are you prepared for this change?"

### Day 1 — Explanation Content
- **"Did You Know?" card (Template 10):** New rate + effective date + one-line impact
- **LinkedIn post:** Professional breakdown — who it affects, what changes, what employers need to do. ~250 words.
- **Facebook post:** Longer, more conversational — "If you're running payroll in Jamaica, here's what the NIS change actually means for your next payroll run..."

### Day 2–3 — Educational Depth
- **Infographic (Template 09 variation):** Before/after comparison of all rates — old vs. new
- **LinkedIn carousel (Template 01):** "What changes, what stays the same, what you need to do by [date]" — 5 slides
- **Instagram carousel (Template 02):** Step-by-step: "Here's how to update your payroll for the new NIS rate"

### Week 2 — Evergreen Follow-up
- **Blog post:** Full guide with updated calculations — becomes a long-term SEO asset
- All blog repurposing assets from Section 1 above (9 additional assets)

**Total assets from one news event:** 12–15 pieces of content over 2 weeks

**Key rule:** Never be alarmist. Be the calm, authoritative voice. "Here's what changed, here's what it means, here's how to handle it."

## 5. One Stat → 5 Formats

**Source stat:** "Jamaican employers pay 9.5% on top of every salary in statutory contributions"

### Format A — Stat Card (Instagram)
- Use Template 03
- "9.5%" in 96pt Solar Gold
- Subline: "What Jamaican employers pay per salary in statutory contributions"
- Caption: "That's NIS (3%) + NHT (3%) + Education Tax (3.5%). Are you calculating this correctly?"

### Format B — Carousel (LinkedIn/Instagram)
- Turn it into "Breaking down the 9.5%" — use Template 01 or 02
- Slide 1: Headline stat
- Slide 2: NIS breakdown
- Slide 3: NHT breakdown
- Slide 4: Education Tax breakdown
- Slide 5: Total cost + "Let us handle the math"

### Format C — Reel Script
- Hook (0–3 sec): "Did you know hiring someone for J$100,000 a month actually costs you J$109,500?"
- Explain (3–30 sec): Break down each 3 components on screen with text
- Payoff (30–50 sec): "Most Jamaican businesses either overpay or underpay this. Here's how to get it right."
- CTA (50–60 sec): "Use our free payroll calculator to see your exact costs."

### Format D — Story (Instagram/Facebook)
- Use Template 07
- Question: "Do you know how much your employer pays on TOP of your salary?"
- Poll: "Yes, exactly / I have no idea"
- Follow-up Story: Reveal the 9.5% breakdown. End with link to calculator.

### Format E — LinkedIn Article / Newsletter Section
- Title: "The hidden cost of employment in Jamaica: understanding the 9.5% employer burden"
- 400–600 word article with context, history of each contribution, and practical advice
- Include a comparison table (statutory vs. total cost of employment)
- CTA: Use PayrollJamaica to see this calculated automatically

**Total from one stat:** 5 distinct assets across 3–4 platforms, ~4–5 hours of production time with templates ready.

## Repurposing Calendar Rhythm

Use this weekly rhythm to stay consistent without burning out:

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| Monday | Identify 1 source piece (blog, stat, news, testimonial) |
| Tuesday | Write all long-form copy variants (LinkedIn posts, captions) |
| Wednesday | Design all graphics using Canva templates (batch in one session) |
| Thursday | Schedule posts across platforms |
| Friday | Monitor engagement and identify the top performer for next week's remix |

**Monthly repurposing audit:** Each month, look at the top 3 performing posts and ask: "Have we fully extracted value from this content?" If not — go back through the matrix and produce the missing formats.
