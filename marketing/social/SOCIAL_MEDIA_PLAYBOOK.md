# PayrollJamaica Social Media Playbook
**Version 1.0 | March 2026**
**Audience: SME owners and HR managers in Jamaica (10–500 employees)**

---

## Brand Voice & Tone

### Core Identity
PayrollJamaica is the **trusted local expert** — not a faceless software company. We speak like the sharp accountant who grew up in Kingston, went to UWI, and built something to solve a real Jamaican problem. We are:

- **Knowledgeable but not condescending** — We explain PAYE like you'd explain it to a smart friend, not a textbook
- **Local and proud** — We reference Jamaican seasons, culture, and regulatory calendar authentically
- **Direct and useful** — Every post earns its place. No filler.
- **Warm with a sense of humor** — Not stiff corporate speak, not trying-too-hard patois

### Voice Spectrum
```
TOO STIFF                    SWEET SPOT                TOO CASUAL
"Leverage our              "Your PAYE                  "Bredda, TAJ
 enterprise-grade          deadline is                  a go lock
 compliance suite"         tomorrow. Here's            you dung lol"
                           how to stay clean."
```

### Patois Usage Rules
- Use patois **sparingly** and only when it adds warmth or humor
- Approved phrases: "no problem", "irie", "sort it out", "likkle more", "one love"
- Never mock or exaggerate patois for comedic effect
- When in doubt, write in standard Jamaican English (not British, not American)
- Patois works best in: cultural celebration posts, humor posts, holiday acknowledgments

### 1. LinkedIn

**Why we're here:** B2B credibility, HR manager reach, SME founder decision-makers, accounting professionals who refer us

**Primary audience:** HR managers, business owners (20–200 employees), accountants, payroll officers

**Tone on LinkedIn:** Professional but human. Think: Harvard Business Review meets Jamaica Observer Business section.

**Content Types & Ratios:**
| Type | % | Examples |
|------|---|---------|
| Educational (how-to, regulatory) | 45% | "How to calculate PAYE step by step" |
| Product features & proof | 25% | Calculator demo, customer result |
| Industry insight | 20% | TAJ updates, NHT rate changes |
| Company culture | 10% | Team, behind-the-scenes |

**Posting Cadence:** 3x per week — Monday, Wednesday, Friday
- Monday: Educational/How-to (catches people planning their week)
- Wednesday: Product or social proof (mid-week decision mode)
- Friday: Industry news or lighter insight (wrapping up, forward-looking)

**Post Format Best Practices:**
- Hook in line 1 — must work before "see more" cut-off (150 characters)
- Use line breaks liberally — dense paragraphs get skipped
- Numbered lists outperform bullet points on LinkedIn
- End with 1 clear question to drive comments
- Ideal length: 150–300 words for body posts; 800–1200 for long-form articles
- Native documents/carousels: post as LinkedIn Document (PDF) for 3–5x more reach
- No link in first comment trick: post the link 30 min after publishing

**LinkedIn Hashtag Strategy:**
- 3–5 hashtags maximum
- Always include: #PayrollJamaica #JamaicaBusiness
- Rotate: #HRJamaica #SMEJamaica #PayrollCompliance #TAJJamaica #JamaicanBusinessOwners

**LinkedIn-Specific Tips:**
- Tag @TaxAdministrationJamaica and @NHT when relevant (increases authority)
- Employees sharing posts dramatically boosts reach — build internal sharing culture
- Polls perform extremely well: run one per month on compliance topics
- Articles (long-form) rank in Google for Jamaica payroll searches — publish monthly

### 2. Facebook

**Why we're here:** Jamaica's most-used platform for SME communities, Facebook Groups are where Jamaican business owners actually gather and ask questions

**Primary audience:** SME owners, managers, HR staff, accountants — all ages, all parishes

**Tone on Facebook:** Friendly neighbor. Like a post in a Jamaica business group — helpful, real, occasionally funny. More casual than LinkedIn.

**Target Facebook Groups to engage in (comment, not just post):**
- Jamaica Business & Entrepreneurs
- Small Business Jamaica
- HR Professionals Jamaica
- Jamaica Accounting Network
- (Parish-specific business groups: Kingston Business Network, etc.)

**Content Types & Ratios:**
| Type | % | Examples |
|------|---|---------|
| Educational tips | 35% | "3 NHT mistakes that get businesses audited" |
| Product/tool promos | 25% | Calculator, free trial CTA |
| Cultural/local moments | 25% | Holidays, cultural references |
| Engagement (polls, questions) | 15% | "How do you currently process payroll?" |

**Posting Cadence:** Daily (7 days/week)
- Monday–Friday: Educational or product posts
- Saturday: Lighter, cultural, or engagement post
- Sunday: Motivational or week-ahead reminder

**Post Format Best Practices:**
- Facebook penalizes link posts in the feed — use native video or image with link in comments
- Images: bright, clean, text-light (Facebook compresses heavy-text images)
- Videos under 90 seconds perform best; vertical (9:16) for mobile
- Use Facebook's "Questions" feature for polls on the Page
- Stories: post daily, can be more casual/raw
- Reels: repurpose TikTok content (without TikTok watermark)

**Facebook Hashtag Strategy:**
- 2–4 hashtags (Facebook hashtags have less impact than Instagram/LinkedIn)
- #PayrollJamaica #JamaicaBusiness #SMEJamaica

## Universal Content Rules

### What We NEVER Post:
- Specific competitor criticism by name
- Political party content or opinions
- Claims we can't back up with regulation citations
- American/British tax references without Jamaica context
- Stock photos of obviously non-Jamaican workplaces

### What We ALWAYS Include:
- A clear call-to-action in every promotional post
- The free calculator URL when relevant: **payrolljamaica.com/calculator**
- Our handle tag when reposting or collaborating: **@PayrollJamaica**

## Crisis Communication Protocol

If TAJ changes rates or issues penalties:
1. Post factual update within 2 hours on all platforms
2. Lead with what businesses need to DO, not just what changed
3. Link to official TAJ source (taj.gov.jm)
4. Offer free calculator update walkthrough
5. Never speculate — only confirmed regulatory facts

## Content Approval Workflow
1. Draft → Internal review (compliance check for any rates/figures)
2. Design (for visual posts) → Final approval
3. Schedule via Buffer/Later minimum 48 hours in advance
4. Emergency posts (regulatory changes): expedited 2-hour review
