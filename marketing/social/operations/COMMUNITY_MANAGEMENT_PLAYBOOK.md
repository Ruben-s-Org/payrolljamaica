# PayrollJamaica — Community Management Playbook
**Audience:** Social media manager, CMO, anyone handling inbound messages
**Goal:** Build authentic presence in the Jamaican business community; convert conversations to leads
**Tone:** Professional but warm. Caribbean business culture values respect and genuine relationship. Never pushy.

### Group Posting Rules (READ BEFORE POSTING)
1. Read each group's pinned post / rules before posting anything promotional
2. Some groups ban promotional content — in those, only share educational content or answer questions
3. Always lead with value, never with a sales pitch
4. Post 3-5 times as a helpful community member before posting anything with a product link
5. Respond to every comment on your posts within 24 hours
6. Never post the same content word-for-word across multiple groups on the same day — paraphrase

When you see someone asking a payroll question, answer it fully and helpfully. End with: "If this is something you deal with regularly, our free calculator at payrolljamaica.com/calculator handles this automatically."

## PART 3: HOW TO HANDLE COMPLAINTS AND DIFFICULT SITUATIONS

### Complaint Type 1: "Your calculator gave me the wrong number"

This is the most critical complaint to handle well. Respond within 1 hour if possible.

**Step 1 — Acknowledge immediately, don't be defensive:**
```
Hi [Name], thank you for flagging this — we take accuracy very seriously. Can you share the details with me? Specifically: the gross salary you entered, the pay period, and what result you got? I want to check this personally and get back to you quickly.
```

**Step 2 — Investigate:** Run the same calculation yourself. Compare against TAJ official rates. Check if the rates in the system match current year.

**Step 3a — If we were wrong:**
```
Hi [Name], you're right — we found the issue. [Explain what was wrong.] We've corrected it and the calculator now shows [correct result]. I'm sorry for the confusion this caused. We've also audited the other calculations and they're correct.

Can I send you the corrected breakdown for your records? And if this affected any payroll you've already processed, I'm happy to help you work through the correction.
```

**Step 3b — If their input was wrong or they misunderstood:**
```
Hi [Name], thanks for the patience while I looked into this. After checking, I believe the calculator result was correct. Here's why:

[Explain the calculation step by step — don't be condescending, be educational]

The confusion often comes from [specific point]. Does this make sense? Happy to get on a call and walk through it together if that would help.
```

**Step 4 — After resolution:**
Internally log the complaint, the root cause, and whether a product/content change is needed. Report in weekly CMO report.

### Complaint Type 2: "I signed up and it's not working"

```
Hi [Name], I'm sorry to hear you're running into issues — let's get this sorted. Can you tell me:
1. What specifically isn't working? (Error message, feature not loading, etc.)
2. What browser / device are you using?
3. What email address did you sign up with?

I'll escalate this to the tech team immediately.
```

Follow up with tech team. Update the customer within 2 hours with a status.

### Complaint Type 3: Negative public comment on a post

Never delete legitimate complaints (it looks worse). Respond publicly:
```
Hi [Name], thanks for the feedback — we want to make this right. I've sent you a DM to sort this out directly. We're listening.
```
Then handle in DM using the relevant script above.

### Crisis Communications — "Calculator gave wrong numbers" goes viral

If a post or tweet gains traction accusing PayrollJamaica of incorrect calculations:

**Immediate actions (within 2 hours):**
1. Screenshot and preserve the post
2. Do the calculation manually — verify whether they are right or wrong
3. Draft a response (have CMO or founder review before posting)
4. If we're wrong: post a clear, honest correction with specific numbers
5. If we're right: post a clear, detailed explanation with the math shown

**Public statement template (if we were wrong):**
```
We found an error in our [specific calculation type] and we're fixing it now.

Here's what happened: [plain language explanation]
Here's the correct calculation: [show the math]
Here's what we've done: [fix deployed, or fix in progress with ETA]

We're sorry. Accuracy is our entire value proposition. If you processed payroll based on incorrect numbers from us, contact us directly and we'll help you correct it.

This is not good enough and we're doing better.

— PayrollJamaica Team
```

**Never:**
- Be defensive or dismissive
- Delete the comments
- Blame the user
- Go silent
- Argue about math in public comments

## PART 5: BUILDING RELATIONSHIPS WITH ACCOUNTANTS AND HR CONSULTANTS

Accountants and HR consultants are a multiplier channel — one accountant who recommends PayrollJamaica can send you 10+ clients.

### How to Identify Them
- Search LinkedIn: "accountant Jamaica", "HR consultant Kingston", "payroll specialist Jamaica"
- Search Facebook groups: admins of Jamaica Accountants Network, HR Professionals Jamaica
- Search Instagram: #JamaicaAccountant #HRJamaica #JamaicaFinance
- Check Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (ACCA) Jamaica chapter

### Relationship Building Sequence (Don't Rush This)

**Week 1-2:** Follow them on all platforms. Like and comment genuinely on their content. Don't mention PayrollJamaica yet.

**Week 3:** Send a connection request or follow with a personal note:
```
Hi [Name], I've been following your content on [platform] — your post about [specific thing] was really useful. I run PayrollJamaica, a payroll platform built for Jamaica businesses. Not reaching out to sell anything — just building relationships in the Jamaica business space. Would love to connect.
```

**Week 4+:** Once connected and warm, share something useful without asking for anything:
```
Hi [Name], saw you mentioned [relevant topic] — thought this article/tool might be useful for your clients: [link]. We've built a free payroll calculator that a few accountants have been using to double-check calculations quickly. No agenda — just thought it might be handy.
```

**After relationship is established:** Ask for feedback, not referrals:
```
Hi [Name], we're improving PayrollJamaica and I'd value your perspective as someone who works with multiple Jamaica businesses. Would you be open to a 15-minute call to give us honest feedback on what you'd want from a payroll tool? Happy to pay for your time.
```

### Partnership Approach
For accountants managing multiple clients, explore a partner/reseller model:
- Partner dashboard to manage multiple client payrolls
- Referral commission or white-label option (future roadmap)
- Co-branded educational content
- "Preferred partner" badge for their website

We're looking to build relationships with Jamaica accountants and bookkeepers who manage payroll for multiple clients. I think there's a real opportunity to work together — whether that's a referral arrangement, shared content, or something else that makes sense for both of us.

## PART 7: HANDLING "HOW MUCH DOES IT COST?" IN DMs

This comes up constantly. Don't dodge it — answer it directly and frame it well.

### Standard Response
```
Great question — here's how it works:

The free payroll calculator at payrolljamaica.com/calculator is completely free, forever. No signup needed. It handles all Jamaica statutory deductions for individual employee calculations.

For the full platform (run full payroll cycles, generate payslips, track multiple employees, compliance reports):
[Insert your actual pricing here]

Most of our users start with the free calculator to see that the numbers are right, then move to the full platform when they're ready.

What's your situation? How many employees are you processing payroll for? I can give you a more specific recommendation.
```

### If They Push Back on Price
```
I completely understand — budget is real. A few things worth knowing:

1. The free calculator will handle a lot of what you need right now — no cost, use it as often as you like.

2. For the full platform, think about what you're spending on payroll today — whether that's your time, an accountant's time, or penalties from errors. [X] per month often pays for itself quickly.

3. If price is a barrier, let's talk — I'd rather find something that works than lose a customer who'd be a great fit.

What's the main thing holding you back?
```

### If They Say "I'll Think About It"
```
Of course — take your time. A couple of things while you think about it:

1. Start with the free calculator today — no commitment, just see if the numbers match what you're used to. payrolljamaica.com/calculator

2. Fiscal year ends March 31 — if you want to start clean for the new fiscal year, the timing is actually good right now.

I'll leave it with you. Reach out anytime.
```
