## CATEGORY 2: JAMAICA HR CONSULTANTS WITH SOCIAL PRESENCE

HR consultants are a critical channel — they advise the exact businesses we serve.

### How to Find Them
- LinkedIn: Search "HR consultant Jamaica", "HR manager Jamaica", "payroll specialist Kingston"
- Facebook: Search for people posting in "HR Professionals Jamaica" group
- Instagram: #HRJamaica #HRConsultantJamaica
- Industry: Caribbean HR Summit attendees (search their speaker list)

### Priority Profiles to Research and Contact

**Type A — Independent HR Consultants**
Individuals who consult with multiple SMEs on HR compliance. They often post content about employment law, payroll, and HR best practices.

LinkedIn search terms to find them:
- "HR consultant" + Jamaica (location filter)
- "Payroll specialist" + Jamaica
- "Employment law" + Jamaica
- "Human resources" + Kingston or Montego Bay

**Type B — HR Managers at Jamaica Companies**
HR managers at mid-sized Jamaica companies. They are both potential customers AND influencers within their professional networks.

Target companies to identify HR managers:
- Digicel Jamaica HR team
- NCB Financial Group HR
- GraceKennedy HR
- Victoria Mutual Group HR
- Sagicor Jamaica HR
- JMMB HR
- Caribbean Producers Jamaica HR

Note: These large companies likely have their own payroll systems, but their HR managers are influential in Jamaica HR circles and can refer smaller businesses.

**Type C — HR Academics / Trainers**
HR professionals who teach or train others. A recommendation from them reaches entire cohorts of new HR managers.
- UTECH Jamaica (University of Technology) HR faculty
- UTech School of Business Administration
- UWI Mona campus — Management Studies faculty
- HEART NSTA Trust — workforce training programs

Accountants are the most direct referral channel for payroll software. One accountant with 20 SME clients = 20 potential PayrollJamaica customers.

### Key Organizations to Work Through

**Institute of Chartered Accountants of Jamaica (ICAJ)**
- Website: icaj.org
- Social: LinkedIn company page, potentially Facebook
- Approach: Offer to present at a chapter event or contribute to their newsletter
- Email: info@icaj.org (verify on their website)

**Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (ACCA) Jamaica**
- ACCA has an active Jamaica chapter
- Reach: Large network of Jamaican accountants in practice
- Approach: ACCA Jamaica events, LinkedIn group, newsletter sponsorship (low cost or free editorial)

**Jamaica Institute of Management (JIM)**
- Offers accounting and financial management training
- Approach: Partner content on payroll compliance

### LinkedIn Search Strategy for Accountants

Run these searches on LinkedIn:
- "accountant" + Jamaica (location)
- "CPA" + Kingston
- "chartered accountant" + Jamaica
- "bookkeeper" + Jamaica
- "auditor" + Jamaica
- "tax consultant" + Kingston

Filter by: 500+ connections (these people have large networks), Content creators (they post regularly)

## CATEGORY 5: JAMAICAN BUSINESS MEDIA

Getting covered in media is the highest-leverage organic activity. One article in Jamaica Observer Business reaches more people than months of social posting.

### Primary Targets

**1. Jamaica Observer — Business Section**
- Website: jamaicaobserver.com
- Business editor: Look for bylines on business section
- Business reporter covering SME/tech: Research current reporters
- Contact: newsroom@jamaicaobserver.com or use contact form
- Pitch angle: "Jamaica's first payroll software built for local compliance — launching as fiscal year ends"
- Secondary angle: "Most Jamaica SMEs are calculating PAYE incorrectly — here's why" (data-driven)

**2. The Gleaner — Business Section**
- Website: jamaica-gleaner.com
- Business section editor
- Contact: feedback@gleanerjm.com or specific reporter emails
- Pitch angle: Same as above — different reporter, different angle
- Note: The Gleaner and Observer are rivals — pitch one before the other, not both simultaneously

**3. Loop Jamaica — Business**
- Website: loopjamaica.com
- More digital-native, faster turnaround than print papers
- More receptive to "startup" and "tech" framing
- Contact: Through website contact form or find reporters on Twitter/LinkedIn
- Pitch angle: "Jamaica startup building payroll tool targets SME market ahead of fiscal year-end"

**4. RJR Business / Nationwide News Network**
- Radio-first but strong digital presence
- Approach: Offer to be a guest on a business radio segment
- Topic pitch: "Getting your Jamaica payroll right before March 31"
- Contact: rjrnewsonline.com

**5. Caribbean Business Report**
- Covers broader Caribbean but Jamaica coverage is significant
- Contact through website
- Pitch: Jamaica-specific payroll compliance product launch

**6. Trade publications / association newsletters**
- PSOJ newsletter (psoj.org.jm)
- JCCI (Jamaica Chamber of Commerce) newsletter
- JMEA (Jamaica Manufacturers) newsletter
- Offer to write a guest article on payroll compliance — these go directly to business owners

### Media Pitch Email Template
```
Subject: Story pitch: Jamaica startup fixing the payroll compliance gap for SMEs

The angle: Most Jamaican SMEs are calculating payroll deductions incorrectly — not out of negligence, but because Jamaica's statutory deduction structure (NIS, NHT, Education Tax, PAYE calculated in a specific order on different bases) is genuinely complex, and until now there was no Jamaica-specific tool to do it correctly.

We've launched PayrollJamaica to solve this — software and a free calculator built specifically for Jamaica compliance. The timing is interesting: the fiscal year ends March 31, so businesses are in the window to fix errors before they file.

I can offer:
- Data on common Jamaica payroll calculation errors (from our beta users)
- On-record quotes from Jamaica business owners or accountants about the problem
- A demo of the tool for your readers
- The full story of why we built this

Would this be a good fit for a piece in the next two weeks? Happy to send a full press release or set up a call.

[Name]
PayrollJamaica | payrolljamaica.com | [phone]
```

### Follow-Up Protocol for Media
- Send pitch email
- Wait 5 business days
- Follow up once: "Wanted to make sure this didn't get lost — happy to adjust the angle if it's not the right fit."
- If no response: try a different reporter at the same outlet, or a different outlet
- Never send to the same reporter more than twice without a response
