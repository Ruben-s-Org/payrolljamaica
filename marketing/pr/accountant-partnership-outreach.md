## Outreach Strategy & Notes

### Where to Find Jamaica Accountants

| Source | Method |
|---|---|
| **ICAJ (Institute of Chartered Accountants of Jamaica)** | icaj.org — member directory; attendees of ICAJ events |
| **TAJ registered tax practitioners** | Tax Administration Jamaica publishes lists of registered practitioners |
| **LinkedIn** | Search "accountant Jamaica", "bookkeeper Jamaica", "payroll Jamaica" — filter by location |
| **Jamaica Yellow Pages / Business Directory** | yellowpages.com.jm — search "accountant" by parish |
| **Google** | "accountant Kingston Jamaica", "bookkeeper Montego Bay" — scrape top results |
| **JBDC network** | JBDC has SME accountant contacts through their advisory programs |
| **Word of mouth** | Ask existing users of PayrollJamaica.com who their accountant is |

### Tone Guidelines for Jamaica Business Outreach

- **Professional but warm**: Jamaica business culture values genuine relationships. Avoid overly corporate or stiff language.
- **Respect their time**: Accountants are busy. Get to the point quickly. The three-paragraph rule applies — lead paragraph, value proposition, clear CTA.
- **Emphasize partnership, not selling**: Accountants are wary of being "used" as sales channels. Frame everything as mutual benefit and client service.
- **Acknowledge the compliance burden**: Show that you understand what they deal with — complex rate calculations, demanding clients, tight deadlines. This builds credibility.
- **Follow up once**: If there's no response to the follow-up, pause and try a different channel (LinkedIn, phone). Don't spam.
