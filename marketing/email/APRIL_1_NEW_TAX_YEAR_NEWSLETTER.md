## What just changed

Every cumulative payroll figure resets to zero today. Year-to-date PAYE, NIS contributions, Education Tax — all back to baseline. That means:

- Your employees' PAYE calculations start fresh from their first April pay period
- The NIS contribution ceiling resets (J$5M annual ceiling — tracking starts over)
- Any salary adjustments effective April 1 need to be reflected in your very first payroll run

If your system still has last year's cumulative balances showing, fix that before you process a single payslip.

## The rates for 2026/2027

Good news: the core statutory rates carry forward. But it's worth confirming you have every number locked in correctly from day one.

| Deduction | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| **PAYE** | 25% (up to J$6M) / 30% (above) | — |
| **PAYE threshold** | J$1,500,096/year (J$125,008/month) | — |
| **NIS** | 3% | 3% |
| **NHT** | 2% | 3% |
| **Education Tax** | 2.25% | 3.5% |
| **HEART/NSTA** | — | 3% |

**Remember:** Employees earning under J$125,008/month gross pay zero PAYE — but NIS, NHT, and Education Tax still apply to every dollar earned. This trips up more businesses than you'd think.

## How to reset your payroll for the new year

Here's your quick-start checklist for today:

1. **Zero out year-to-date balances** — cumulative PAYE, NIS, NHT, Ed Tax should all read zero for every employee as of April 1
2. **Verify employee records** — TRN, NIS number, NHT number all current? Any new hires or terminations to reflect?
3. **Confirm deduction rates** — use the table above. If even one rate is wrong, every payslip this year will be off
4. **Apply April salary changes** — if anyone got a raise effective this tax year, update it before your first payroll run
5. **Set your May 14 reminder** — your first S01 is due in 6 weeks. Don't let it sneak up on you
6. **Run a test calculation** — process one employee through your system and verify the numbers before going live
