## Before You Run Your First Payroll

### Employer Registration (Do Once)
- [ ] Register with Tax Administration Jamaica (TAJ) for PAYE — form IT01 at any TAJ office or online at [taj.gov.jm](https://www.taj.gov.jm)
- [ ] Register with the National Insurance Scheme (NIS) — form SS1 at any NIS office
- [ ] Register with the National Housing Trust (NHT) — form R1 at any NHT office or online at [nht.gov.jm](https://www.nht.gov.jm)
- [ ] Register as an employer with the Human Employment and Resource Training (HEART) Trust/NTA if you have payroll above JMD $173,000/month
- [ ] Obtain Taxpayer Registration Number (TRN) for your business if not already done
- [ ] Open a dedicated bank account for statutory remittances (recommended)

## 2026 Statutory Rates Reference Card

| Deduction | Employee Rate | Employer Rate | Ceiling |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PAYE Income Tax** | 25% (up to threshold + $4.5M) / 30% (above) | — | None |
| **NIS** | 3% | 3% | JMD $5,000,000/year |
| **NHT** | 2% | 3% | None |
| **Education Tax** | 2.25% | 3.5% | None |
| **HEART** | — | 3% | Employers with >$173k/mo payroll |

**Income Tax Threshold (2026):** JMD $1,902,360/year (JMD $158,530/month) — effective April 2026

## Monthly Payroll Checklist (Every Pay Period)

### Before Payroll Runs
- [ ] Verify employee hours/days worked for the period
- [ ] Confirm any new hires — collect TD1 form (income tax declaration) from each new employee
- [ ] Confirm any terminations — calculate final pay including statutory notice pay and any accrued benefits
- [ ] Check for any salary changes, bonuses, or commissions
- [ ] Confirm current NIS ceiling has not been reached for any employee (max JMD $416,667/month)

### Calculate Deductions
- [ ] Calculate PAYE using current threshold (JMD $158,530/month for 2026)
- [ ] Calculate NIS at 3% employee / 3% employer (ceiling: JMD $416,667 gross/month)
- [ ] Calculate NHT at 2% employee / 3% employer (no ceiling)
- [ ] Calculate Education Tax at 2.25% employee / 3.5% employer (no ceiling)
- [ ] Calculate HEART at 3% employer if applicable

### Process and Pay
- [ ] Generate payslips for every employee (required by law)
- [ ] Payslip must show: gross pay, each deduction itemized, net pay
- [ ] Remit employee net pay to bank accounts or via cheque

## Monthly Remittance Deadlines

> ⚠️ **PAYE, NIS, NHT, and Education Tax must be remitted by the 14th of the following month.**
> Late payments attract a 10% penalty + interest.

| Month Worked | Remittance Deadline |
|---|---|
| January | February 14 |
| February | March 14 |
| March | April 14 |
| April | May 14 |
| May | June 14 |
| June | July 14 |
| July | August 14 |
| August | September 14 |
| September | October 14 |
| October | November 14 |
| November | December 14 |
| December | January 14 (following year) |

**Where to remit:**
- TAJ (PAYE + Education Tax): Online via TAJ eServices, any TAJ office, or authorized bank
- NIS: Any NIS office or approved bank using your Employer Registration Number
- NHT: Online via NHT portal, any NHT office, or approved bank

## Quarterly Filing

- [ ] **March 31** — S02 Annual Return of Earnings deadline (report total gross pay and PAYE withheld per employee for the prior year)
- [ ] **March 31** — P45 (Employer's Annual Statement) filed with TAJ
- [ ] NHT contributions reviewed — ensure no employee was over-deducted

---

## Annual Year-End Checklist

### Before March 31 Each Year
- [ ] Prepare S02 form for each employee showing: name, TRN, gross earnings, total PAYE deducted for the year
- [ ] Reconcile total PAYE remitted monthly against S02 amounts — any shortfall must be paid
- [ ] Issue P24 certificates (Annual PAYE Certificate) to each employee
- [ ] Review for any employees who may have overpaid PAYE and initiate refunds
- [ ] Confirm NIS ceiling was correctly applied for high earners
- [ ] File all forms with TAJ by March 31 to avoid late filing penalties

---

## New Employee Onboarding Payroll Checklist

- [ ] Collect TD1 (Income Tax Declaration) form — determines correct PAYE threshold
- [ ] Collect TRN (Taxpayer Registration Number) — required for NIS/NHT/PAYE remittances
- [ ] Collect NIS number — required for NIS contributions
- [ ] Collect NHT number — required for NHT contributions
- [ ] Confirm employment type: full-time, part-time, contract — affects statutory obligations
- [ ] Issue employment contract (written contract required under Jamaica Employment Act)
- [ ] Set up employee in payroll system with correct bank account details
- [ ] Explain payslip format to employee

## Record-Keeping Requirements

> Jamaican law requires you to keep payroll records for **7 years**.

Keep on file:
- [ ] All payslips and payroll registers
- [ ] TD1 forms for all employees
- [ ] All NIS/NHT/Education Tax/PAYE remittance receipts
- [ ] S02 and P24 year-end filings
- [ ] Employment contracts and termination records

## Common Compliance Mistakes to Avoid

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correct Approach |
|---|---|
| Using last year's tax threshold | Update PAYE calculation when new Budget is announced (April each year) |
| Forgetting Education Tax | It applies to every employee, every payroll |
| Missing the 14th remittance deadline | Set a recurring calendar reminder for the 13th |
| Not issuing payslips | Required by law — generate for every employee, every pay period |
| Missing the March 31 S02 deadline | Prepare from January — don't leave it until March |
| Treating contractors as employees | Contractors have different statutory obligations — get legal advice if unsure |
