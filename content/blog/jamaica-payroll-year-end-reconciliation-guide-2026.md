# Jamaica Payroll Year-End Reconciliation 2026: The Complete Employer Guide

**Meta description:** Step-by-step guide to Jamaica payroll year-end reconciliation before March 31. Covers SO1, NHT, NIS, P6 forms, and how to fix errors before the TAJ deadline.

Year-end reconciliation is the process of confirming that every payroll deduction made during the 2025/2026 fiscal year was calculated correctly, remitted on time, and reported accurately to TAJ, NHT, and NIS.

Get it right and your March 31 filings are clean. Get it wrong and you're looking at TAJ assessments, NHT penalties, and potentially months of back-and-forth with the authorities.

This guide walks you through the full reconciliation process — from pulling your records to submitting your final returns.

---

## Why Year-End Reconciliation Matters

Jamaica's payroll compliance isn't a set-it-and-forget-it affair. PAYE, NHT, NIS, Education Tax, and HEART are all calculated and remitted monthly, but the annual return is when TAJ and NHT verify that every month added up correctly.

The three scenarios that create problems:

1. **Calculation errors** — Wrong PAYE threshold, wrong NIS rate, missing months
2. **Remittance gaps** — Months where payment was made but the S01 wasn't filed correctly
3. **Rate errors** — Using outdated statutory rates (this is very common after Budget changes)

A clean year-end reconciliation closes these gaps before TAJ finds them.

## The Reconciliation Checklist: Step by Step

### Step 1: Pull Your Monthly Payroll Records

For each month from April 2025 to March 2026:
- Total gross wages paid
- Total PAYE deducted
- Total NIS employee + employer contributions
- Total NHT employee + employer contributions
- Total Education Tax (employee + employer)
- HEART (employer only — 3% of gross)

If this information is in different spreadsheets or files, consolidate it now. You'll need a single view of the full year.

### Step 2: Verify the PAYE Threshold

The current annual tax-free threshold is **J$1,500,096** (J$125,008/month).

**Check:** Was this applied correctly from April 1, 2025? If you were using a different threshold number — particularly an outdated figure from a previous fiscal year — your PAYE calculations are wrong.

If the threshold was incorrect, you may have:
- **Over-deducted PAYE** (employees are owed a refund via their personal tax return)
- **Under-deducted PAYE** (you may have a liability)

Correct the figures before filing the annual return.

### Step 3: Cross-Check Monthly Remittances

Compare your payroll records to your TAJ remittance receipts month by month:

| Month | Payroll PAYE | TAJ Receipt | Match? |
|-------|-------------|-------------|--------|
| April 2025 | J$__ | J$__ | ✅ / ❌ |
| May 2025 | J$__ | J$__ | ✅ / ❌ |
| ... | | | |

Any month where the numbers don't match needs to be investigated before you file the annual return. A discrepancy in your SO1 return will flag automatically in TAJ's system.

### Step 4: Reconcile NHT Contributions

Check that NHT contributions (2% employee + 3% employer) were paid for every employee every month. NHT's system is sophisticated — they will catch gaps.

Also check: did any employee leave during the year? Their contributions still need to appear on the NHT annual return for the months they worked.

### Step 5: Check NIS Against the Ceiling

NIS contributions (3% employee + 3% employer) are capped once an employee's annual earnings hit approximately **J$5 million**. At that point, NIS contributions stop for that employee for the rest of the year.

If any employee earned over J$5M, check that contributions stopped at the ceiling — continuing to deduct past the ceiling is an error that employees are entitled to have corrected.

### Step 6: Confirm Education Tax and HEART

Education Tax: 2.25% employee + 3.5% employer of gross wages.
HEART: 3% employer only — often overlooked on manual payrolls.

Both must be included in your annual totals. HEART is easy to miss if your payroll software doesn't handle it automatically.

### Step 7: Produce P6 Forms

Before March 31, produce a P6 form for every employee. This wage certificate shows:
- Total gross earnings
- Total PAYE deducted
- Total NIS (employee portion)
- Total NHT (employee portion)
- Total Education Tax

Employees need P6 forms to file their personal income tax by **April 14**. It's a legal obligation — you must provide these.

## Filing the Annual Returns

Once your reconciliation is complete:

1. **SO1 Annual Return** → File at [etaj.gov.jm](https://etaj.gov.jm) by March 31
2. **NHT Annual Return** → File at [nht.gov.jm](https://www.nht.gov.jm) by March 31
3. **NIS** → Contact NIS directly for annual reconciliation if needed

Both TAJ and NHT portals get busy on March 31. File as early in the day as possible — ideally before 11am.

---

## What If You Find Errors?

If your reconciliation reveals errors before you file, you have options:

**Under-remitted PAYE:** Pay the difference when you file. Voluntary disclosure ahead of a TAJ assessment is treated more favourably.

**Missed NHT months:** Contact NHT directly before March 31. They can often arrange payment arrangements for genuine errors.

**Wrong thresholds:** Correct the calculations in your records and file the accurate numbers. TAJ may send follow-up questions but accurate filing is always the right approach.

What you don't want: filing an annual return you know is wrong. TAJ's cross-checking capability has improved significantly. They will find it.
