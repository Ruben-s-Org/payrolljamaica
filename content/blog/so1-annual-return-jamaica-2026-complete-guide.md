# SO1 Annual Return Jamaica 2026: Complete Guide for Employers

**Meta description:** How to file the SO1 Annual Return in Jamaica before March 31. Step-by-step guide covering TAJ eTAJ portal, deadlines, penalties, and what to include.

Every Jamaican employer must file an SO1 Annual Return with the Tax Administration Jamaica (TAJ) by March 31 each year. It's one of the most important compliance obligations in Jamaica's payroll calendar — and one of the most commonly botched.

This guide covers exactly what the SO1 is, what you need to prepare, how to file, and how to avoid the penalties that catch hundreds of Jamaican businesses every year.

---

## When Is the SO1 Annual Return Deadline in Jamaica?

The SO1 Annual Return must be filed with Tax Administration Jamaica (TAJ) by March 31 each year. This statutory return summarizes all employee earnings and deductions for the tax year ending March 31.

---

## What is the SO1 Annual Return?

The SO1 (also written as S01) is the employer's annual summary of statutory deductions remitted to TAJ throughout the fiscal year. It reconciles your monthly S01 remittances (which you should have filed every month) with the annual totals for each employee.

Think of it as TAJ cross-checking your year's payroll work. If your annual return doesn't match your monthly remittances, you'll hear about it.

**What the SO1 covers:**
- Total gross emoluments paid to each employee
- Total PAYE deducted and remitted
- Total NIS contributions (employee and employer)
- Total NHT contributions (employee and employer)
- Total Education Tax deducted
- Total HEART contributions (employer only)

---

## SO1 Annual Return Deadline: March 31

The deadline is fixed: **March 31 each year**, coinciding with the end of Jamaica's fiscal year.

Missing this deadline triggers automatic penalties. There is no grace period.

| Penalty | Amount |
|---------|--------|
| Late filing | J$10,000 per employee on the return |
| Incorrect return | Interest on underpaid tax at TAJ statutory rate |
| Non-filing | TAJ estimated assessment (can exceed actual liability) |

## What You Need Before You File

Before logging into the eTAJ portal, have these ready:

**1. Monthly S01 remittance receipts**
Every month you should have received a receipt or reference number when you paid PAYE/NIS/NHT/Education Tax. Gather these for the full year: April 2025 through March 2026.

**2. Payroll records by employee**
For each employee, you need their total gross pay, and the exact deductions applied each month. If your records are in a spreadsheet, this will take time. If you use PayrollJamaica, these reports are generated automatically.

**3. Employer registration number (ERN)**
Your TAJ Employer Registration Number. If you've lost this, contact TAJ at 888-TAX-HELP.

**4. Employee TRN numbers**
Each employee's Tax Registration Number. These go on the return. Missing TRNs can delay processing.

**5. Final reconciled figures**
Before submitting, cross-check your totals:
- PAYE deducted from employees = PAYE remitted to TAJ each month
- NHT employer (3%) + employee (2%) totals match the number of pay periods × payroll
- NIS contributions don't exceed the annual ceiling (J$1.5M earnings)

## How to File the SO1: eTAJ Portal (Step-by-Step)

The SO1 Annual Return is filed electronically through TAJ's eTAJ portal.

**Step 1:** Go to [etaj.gov.jm](https://etaj.gov.jm) and log in with your employer credentials. If you don't have an eTAJ account, you'll need to register or visit a TAJ office in person.

**Step 2:** From the dashboard, select **"File a Return"** → **"Employer Annual Return (SO1)"**.

**Step 3:** Select the tax year: **2025/2026** (April 1, 2025 – March 31, 2026).

**Step 4:** Enter or upload employee data. The portal accepts manual entry or a CSV upload for larger employers.

**Step 5:** Review the auto-calculated totals. The system will flag discrepancies between your annual return and what's already on file from your monthly remittances.

**Step 6:** Submit and save your confirmation number.

**Pro tip:** File before 2pm on March 31. The eTAJ portal gets congested in the afternoon as the deadline approaches. System slowdowns are common on March 31 — file early.

## PAYE Threshold for 2025/2026

The current annual income tax threshold is **J$1,500,096** (J$125,008/month). Any employee earning below this threshold has no PAYE liability.

If you've been calculating PAYE on the wrong threshold — a common error from prior years when the threshold was different — your annual return will show a discrepancy. Correct this before filing.

---

## Common SO1 Mistakes to Avoid

**1. Wrong PAYE threshold**
Many employers are still using an outdated figure. The current threshold is J$1,500,096. If your PAYE calculations used a different number, your return will have errors.

**2. Missing employees**
Every employee who received a salary or wages during the fiscal year must appear on the return — including employees who left during the year.

**3. NHT employer rate error**
The employer NHT rate is **3%** (not 2%). The employee rate is 2%. Both go on the return. Swapping these is a common error.

**4. Forgetting HEART**
HEART Tax (Human Employment and Resource Training) is **3% employer-only** on gross wages. It often gets left off when employers calculate PAYE manually.

**5. Not reconciling with monthly remittances first**
Filing an annual return that contradicts your monthly payments will trigger a TAJ review. Reconcile before you submit.

---

## What Happens After You File?

TAJ will process your return and compare it against monthly remittances received. If there are no discrepancies, your account is marked as compliant for the fiscal year.

If there are discrepancies, TAJ may:
- Issue a notice of assessment for underpaid amounts
- Request supporting documentation
- In serious cases, initiate a formal audit
