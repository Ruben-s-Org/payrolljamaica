# How to Register as a New Employer in Jamaica: Complete 2026 Guide

You've started a business, made your first hire, and now you need to figure out what the Jamaican government actually requires of you as an employer. The list is longer than most people expect — and getting it wrong from the start creates a backlog of penalties and interest that can take years to unwind.

This guide covers every registration you need as a new employer in Jamaica: TAJ (PAYE), NIS, NHT, Education Tax, and the HEART Trust/NTA levy. We'll tell you exactly what to do, in what order, and where to go for each one.

---

## Why Employer Registration Matters

In Jamaica, employers are legally responsible for withholding statutory deductions from their employees' wages and remitting those deductions — plus their own employer contributions — to the relevant agencies every month. You cannot do this without first registering.

Operating payroll without the proper registrations exposes you to:

- **Backdated tax assessments** from the Tax Administration Jamaica
- **Penalties and interest** from NIS, NHT, and TAJ
- **Personal liability** for directors and owners in some cases
- **Disqualified employees** — NHT contributors who cannot access their benefits because their employer never properly registered them

The good news: the registration process, while bureaucratic, is entirely manageable if you go in order. Here's how.

---

## Step 1: Get Your Business TRN from TAJ

Before anything else, your business needs a Tax Registration Number (TRN). If you're registering as a sole trader using your personal TRN, you may already have this — but most businesses, especially limited liability companies and partnerships, need a separate business TRN.

**Who you'll deal with:** Tax Administration Jamaica (TAJ)

**What you need:**
- Certificate of Incorporation (for companies) or Partnership Agreement
- Government-issued ID for directors/owners
- Proof of business address (utility bill or lease agreement)
- Completed Form C (Company Registration) or Form R (for individuals)

**Where to go:**
- In person at any TAJ Revenue Service Centre (Kingston, Montego Bay, Mandeville, and other parish capitals)
- Online at [https://www.jamaicatax.gov.jm](https://www.jamaicatax.gov.jm) via the e-Services portal

**Processing time:** Same day for walk-ins in most cases; 3–5 business days online.

**What you receive:** An Employer TRN that you will use for all subsequent statutory registrations and monthly remittances.

> **Note:** The TRN is a permanent number. It follows the business for its lifetime and is required on every payroll filing, employee payslip, and statutory remittance.

---

## Step 2: Register for PAYE with TAJ

Once you have your Employer TRN, you need to register specifically as a PAYE (Pay As You Earn) employer. This registration activates your obligation to withhold income tax from employees' wages and file monthly S01 returns.

**What PAYE registration means:**
- You become responsible for calculating income tax owed by each employee each pay period
- You withhold that tax from their pay and remit it to TAJ by the 14th of the following month
- You file a monthly S01 (Employer's Monthly Return) even in months where no tax is owed
- At year-end, you issue P24 certificates to each employee and file an annual return

**Annual income tax threshold (2026):** $1,500,096 — employees earning below this amount pay no income tax. Above that, the rate is 25% up to $6,000,000 in annual income, and 30% on the portion above $6,000,000.

**Register at:** [https://www.jamaicatax.gov.jm](https://www.jamaicatax.gov.jm) or any TAJ office.

---

## Step 3: Register with the National Insurance Scheme (NIS)

The National Insurance Scheme provides contributors with retirement benefits, sickness benefits, and death benefits. Both employer and employee contribute.

**Who you'll deal with:** Ministry of Labour and Social Security — NIS Division

**2026 NIS Contribution Rates:**
- **Employee:** 3% of insurable earnings
- **Employer:** 3% of insurable earnings
- **Maximum insurable wage ceiling:** $5,000,000 per year (contributions are capped above this)

**What you need to register:**
- Employer TRN
- Certificate of Incorporation or business registration documents
- List of current employees with their NIS numbers (if they already have them)
- Details of the nature of your business

**Where to register:**
- NIS offices across Jamaica (Kingston head office: 6 Constant Spring Road)
- Online at [https://www.nis.gov.jm](https://www.nis.gov.jm)

**What you receive:** An NIS Employer Registration Number that goes on every employee's contribution record.

**Ongoing obligation:** Submit monthly NIS contribution schedules alongside your payment. Contributions are due by the 14th of the following month, same as PAYE.

> **Important:** If an employee doesn't yet have an NIS number, they can apply directly at any NIS office or through the online portal. You as the employer cannot apply on their behalf, but you should encourage all new hires to get their NIS number before their first payday.

---

## Step 4: Register with the National Housing Trust (NHT)

The NHT funds housing loans for Jamaican workers. Again, both employer and employee contribute, and registration is mandatory once you employ anyone — even part-time workers in many cases.

**Who you'll deal with:** National Housing Trust

**2026 NHT Contribution Rates:**
- **Employee:** 2% of gross wages
- **Employer:** 3% of gross wages (employer pays more than the employee)

**What you need to register:**
- Employer TRN
- Business registration documents
- Names and TRNs of your employees

**Where to register:**
- In person at any NHT office (head office: 4 Park Boulevard, Kingston 5)
- Online at [https://www.nhttrust.com](https://www.nhttrust.com)

**What you receive:** An NHT Employer Number.

**Ongoing obligation:** Submit monthly NHT contribution schedules with payment. Employers who fall behind on NHT remittances face interest charges and can be barred from certain government contracts.

> **Why it matters to employees:** NHT contributions accumulate and employees can use them to access housing loans or refunds after seven years of contributions. If you fail to register and remit, your employees lose out on benefits they think they're earning.

---

## Step 5: Education Tax — No Separate Registration Required

Education Tax is collected alongside PAYE through the TAJ system. Once you're registered as a PAYE employer, you're automatically captured for Education Tax.

**2026 Education Tax Rates:**
- **Employee:** 2.25% of gross emoluments
- **Employer:** 3.5% of gross emoluments

Education Tax applies to all earnings — there is no threshold like PAYE. Even employees who earn below the income tax threshold still owe Education Tax.

It is remitted monthly on the same S01 return as PAYE and NHT (TAJ serves as the collection point for Education Tax on behalf of the Ministry of Education).

---

## Step 6: HEART Trust/NTA Levy

The HEART Trust/NTA (now styled HEART/NSTA Trust) funds vocational training across Jamaica. This is an employer-only contribution — employees do not contribute.

**2026 HEART Levy Rate:**
- **Employer only:** 3% of total gross payroll
- **Threshold:** Applies to employers with an annual payroll above $173,328. Micro-employers below this amount are exempt.

**Registration:** HEART contributions are remitted through TAJ alongside your PAYE and Education Tax. There is no separate HEART registration — it is captured in your PAYE employer registration.

**Ongoing obligation:** The HEART levy is reported on the monthly S01 return and remitted with your other statutory payments.

---

## Putting It All Together: Your Monthly Remittance Deadline

Once all registrations are complete, your ongoing obligation is straightforward in structure, even if it requires careful calculation each month:

| Agency | What you remit | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| TAJ (PAYE + Education Tax + HEART) | Employee PAYE + Ed Tax; Employer Ed Tax + HEART levy | 14th of following month |
| TAJ (on behalf of NHT) | Employee NHT 2% + Employer NHT 3% | 14th of following month |
| NIS | Employee NIS 3% + Employer NIS 3% | 14th of following month |

Everything is due on the **14th of the month following the pay period**. A payroll run in March means remittances due by April 14th.

## Common Mistakes New Employers Make

**Registering late and then trying to backdate.** TAJ, NIS, and NHT all require you to remit from the first month you employed someone — not the month you got around to registering. If there's a gap, you'll owe back contributions plus interest.

**Mixing up employee and employer contributions.** Both NIS and NHT have both an employee-side and an employer-side contribution. You withhold the employee portion from their wages; you fund the employer portion yourself. These are separate obligations.

**Treating PAYE as optional for low earners.** Even employees below the $1,500,096 income tax threshold still owe NIS, NHT, and Education Tax. Many new employers mistakenly think "under the threshold" means no deductions at all.

**Missing the annual return.** Beyond monthly S01 filings, employers must file an annual return and issue P24 certificates to all employees by March 31 each year (covering the prior April–March tax year). This is a separate obligation that catches many first-year employers off guard.

## Registration Checklist

Use this to track where you are:

- [ ] Business TRN obtained from TAJ
- [ ] PAYE employer registration activated with TAJ
- [ ] NIS employer registration completed ([nis.gov.jm](https://www.nis.gov.jm))
- [ ] NHT employer registration completed ([nhttrust.com](https://www.nhttrust.com))
- [ ] Education Tax: automatically captured via PAYE registration
- [ ] HEART levy: automatically captured via PAYE registration
- [ ] All employees have their own NIS numbers and TRNs
- [ ] Payroll process set up to calculate and withhold monthly deductions
- [ ] Reminder set for the 14th of each month for all remittances
- [ ] Annual return and P24 deadline (March 31) in your calendar
