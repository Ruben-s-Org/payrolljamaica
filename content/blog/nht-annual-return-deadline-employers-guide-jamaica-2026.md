# NHT Annual Return Jamaica 2026: Employer Deadline Guide

**Meta description:** NHT Annual Return deadline is March 31. Guide for Jamaican employers on how to file, what to include, the 20% late penalty, and common mistakes to avoid.

The National Housing Trust (NHT) Annual Return is one of two major year-end employer obligations in Jamaica — alongside the TAJ SO1. The NHT return is due by **March 31**, and the penalty for missing it is one of the harshest in Jamaica's statutory compliance landscape: **20% of contributions due**.

This guide tells you exactly what to file, how to file it, and what to watch out for.

---

## Why the NHT Annual Return Matters

Every Jamaican employer must file an annual reconciliation with the NHT confirming total contributions paid for each employee during the fiscal year. This return feeds into each employee's NHT account — which determines how much they can borrow for housing.

If you underpay, file late, or fail to file, the consequences are immediate and financial:

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Late filing | **20% of contributions due** (no cap) |
| Underpayment | 20% surcharge + interest |
| Non-filing | NHT can pursue legal action |

The 20% penalty is not negotiable and applies from April 1. NHT enforces this consistently — more aggressively than many other statutory bodies.

---

## NHT Contribution Rates (2025/2026)

| Contributor | Rate | Basis |
|-------------|------|-------|
| Employee | 2% | Gross pay |
| Employer | 3% | Gross pay |

Both must be included in your annual return. Total per employee: 5% of gross wages for the year.

There is no income ceiling on NHT contributions — unlike NIS, contributions continue regardless of how high the employee's salary goes.

---

## What You Need to Prepare

Before logging into the NHT employer portal, gather:

1. **Total gross wages paid to each employee** — April 2025 through March 2026
2. **Total NHT contributions remitted monthly** — 2% employee + 3% employer each month
3. **Employee NIS numbers** — NHT uses these to match employee accounts
4. **Payment receipts or references** — from each monthly NHT remittance

If you missed any monthly NHT payments during the year, the gap will show in your annual return. Address these before filing — voluntary disclosure is treated more favourably than a NHT-detected discrepancy.

## How to File: NHT Employer Portal

**Step 1:** Go to [nht.gov.jm](https://www.nht.gov.jm) and log into the employer section.

**Step 2:** Navigate to **Annual Returns** → **2025/2026 Annual Return**.

**Step 3:** Enter or upload employee contribution data. For employers with fewer than 20 employees, manual entry works fine. Larger employers should use the CSV upload template available on the portal.

**Step 4:** The portal will calculate employer and employee contribution totals based on your entries. Cross-check against your records.

**Step 5:** Submit and print or save your confirmation.

**Filing tip:** The NHT portal is generally more stable than eTAJ on March 31. Still, file early — aim for March 28 (Friday) or morning of March 31.

## P6 Forms: Your March 31 Companion Obligation

When you file the NHT Annual Return, you also need to produce **P6 forms** for each employee. These wage certificates summarize each employee's:
- Total gross earnings for the year
- Total PAYE deducted
- Total NIS contributions
- Total NHT contributions
- Total Education Tax

Your employees need P6 forms to file their own personal income tax return. The employee filing deadline is **April 14** — they cannot file without the P6 you give them.

## Monthly NHT Filing (S01) vs Annual Return

Don't confuse the two:

| | Monthly S01 | Annual Return |
|---|---|---|
| Filed with | TAJ (via eTAJ) | NHT (via nht.gov.jm) |
| Deadline | 14th of following month | March 31 |
| Covers | PAYE + NIS + NHT + Ed Tax | NHT only |

Your monthly NHT contributions are included in the TAJ S01 remittance, but the NHT Annual Return is a **separate** filing directly with NHT. Both must be completed.

---

## Common Mistakes

**1. Assuming the S01 covers NHT separately**
The monthly TAJ S01 includes NHT contributions, but the annual NHT return is a distinct filing. Many employers do one and forget the other.

**2. Wrong employer rate**
NHT employer rate is **3%** — not 2%. The employee rate is 2%. Both must appear on the return. A common error is using 2% for both.

**3. Missing new hires or terminated employees**
Everyone on payroll during the fiscal year must appear on the return — including staff who left during the year. NHT uses this to credit individual accounts.

**4. Not reconciling contributions first**
If monthly payments don't match the annual totals, NHT will follow up. Reconcile before you submit.
