# Payroll Services in Jamaica 2026: Full Guide for Business Owners

Managing payroll in Jamaica involves significantly more than calculating what employees are owed. Every employer with staff on payroll must correctly handle PAYE withholding, NHT contributions, NIS deductions, and Education Tax — remitting each to the correct government agency on time, every month, with accurate records ready for TAJ review at any point.

For many business owners, this is not where they want to spend their time. That's where payroll services come in.

This guide breaks down the full landscape of payroll services available to Jamaican businesses in 2026: what's included, what different service levels cost in JMD, when to outsource versus handle in-house, and how to evaluate your options.

---

## What "Payroll Services" Actually Covers in Jamaica

When a Jamaican business talks about payroll services, they're typically referring to one or more of the following:

### Payroll Calculation
Computing gross pay, applying all deductions (statutory and voluntary), and arriving at net pay for each employee. In Jamaica, this means correctly applying:
- **PAYE (Pay As You Earn):** Income tax withheld at source. For 2026, the annual tax-free threshold is J$1,500,096. Income above that is taxed at 25% up to J$6,000,000, then 30%.
- **NHT (National Housing Trust):** 2% of gross wages from the employee, plus 3% employer contribution.
- **NIS (National Insurance Scheme):** 3% employee / 3% employer on insurable wages up to J$1,500,000 per annum.
- **Education Tax:** 2.25% employee / 3.5% employer on gross income.

### Payslip Generation
Producing clear, compliant payslips for each employee showing gross pay, itemised statutory deductions, and net pay. Some providers offer digital payslips delivered directly to employees via email or an employee portal.

### Statutory Remittance and Filing
Completing and submitting the required returns:
- **Monthly S01 Return** to the TAJ — due within 14 days of month-end
- **Monthly NHT Return** — due by the 14th of the following month
- **Annual P24 Employer Declaration**
- **Employee P45s** on offboarding
- **Annual NHT Return** — due March 31

## Types of Payroll Services Available in Jamaica

### 1. DIY Payroll Software
You use a software platform to run payroll yourself. The software automates calculations and generates payslips — but you're responsible for the actual filing and payments.

**Best for:** Business owners comfortable with compliance who want full control at the lowest cost.
**Typical cost:** J$1,500–J$2,500 per employee per month.

### 2. Payroll Software with Compliance Automation
The software calculates payroll AND automatically handles statutory filings — generating returns, flagging payment deadlines, and providing exact figures to remit.

**Best for:** Business owners who want compliance accuracy without managing every deadline manually.
**Typical cost:** J$2,500–J$4,000 per employee per month.

### 3. Fully Managed Payroll Service
You send your payroll data and a payroll company handles everything: calculations, payslips, filings, and remittances on your behalf.

**Best for:** Business owners who want to completely delegate payroll, especially those with complex structures.
**Typical cost:** J$4,000–J$8,000 per employee per month.

### 4. In-House Payroll Administrator
Hiring a dedicated payroll administrator to handle payroll manually or using basic tools.

**Best for:** Large businesses (50+ employees) where volume justifies dedicated headcount.
**Cost reality:** J$150,000–J$250,000/month in salary alone, plus employer NHT/NIS (6% on top), plus software costs. For businesses under 30 employees, this almost never makes financial sense.

## Jamaica Payroll Compliance: The Penalties for Getting It Wrong

| Violation | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Late NHT remittance | 20% per annum on outstanding amount |
| Late PAYE remittance | Interest at prescribed rate + potential prosecution |
| Incorrect P24 filing | TAJ assessment + back-payment demand |
| Missing S01 returns | Fines and TAJ audit trigger |

For a business with 20 employees and a monthly PAYE obligation of J$300,000, a single missed month's remittance can cost J$60,000 in annual penalties.

## When to Outsource Payroll vs. Handle In-House

**Outsource payroll if:**
- You have fewer than 30 employees
- You've had compliance issues in the past — missed filings, incorrect PAYE, NHT overpayments
- Your payroll is complex (commissions, multiple pay cycles, contractors)
- You want to reduce personal liability for compliance errors

**Handle in-house if:**
- You have 50+ employees and the volume justifies dedicated headcount
- You have a proven HR/accounts team with strong payroll experience
- Your payroll is simple and consistent month to month
