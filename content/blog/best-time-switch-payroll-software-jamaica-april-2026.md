## The Real Cost of Excel Payroll at Year-End

Most Jamaican SMEs do not abandon Excel because it seems to work — until it does not. Year-end is when the cracks show:

**Formula drift.** A cell reference breaks in March and no one notices until the TAJ reconciliation does not balance. You spend three evenings backtracking through twelve months of data.

**Manual threshold management.** PAYE calculations depend on cumulative annual earnings. Managing that correctly across every employee, every month, in a shared spreadsheet is the kind of task that looks simple until someone changes a formula on row 47.

**S01 filing stress.** The monthly S01 return requires accurate totals for every employee across every statutory deduction. If your spreadsheet has even one error, your filing is wrong. TAJ does not accept "close enough."

**No audit trail.** When a previous employee disputes their tax deductions from eight months ago, Excel gives you nothing. A proper payroll system gives you the complete history.

The labour cost of all this — the hours, the corrections, the anxiety — typically exceeds the cost of software many times over. Most business owners do not notice because they absorb it themselves.
