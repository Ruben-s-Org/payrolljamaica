## What Does Manual Payroll Cost a Small Jamaican Business?

"Manual payroll" means different things to different businesses. It might be a spreadsheet you manage yourself, an accountant who handles it monthly, or a part-time bookkeeper. All of these have real costs — many of which business owners don't fully account for.

### Option 1: Owner Does It Themselves

Time cost is real cost. If you spend 4–6 hours per month running payroll manually — calculating PAYE, NIS, NHT, Education Tax, preparing payslips, remitting to TAJ by the 14th — that's time not spent running your business.

At a conservative value of J$5,000/hour for an owner's time:

- 5 hours/month × J$5,000 = **J$25,000/month in opportunity cost**
- **J$300,000/year** — plus the stress of making sure numbers are right

And that doesn't account for the **March 31 year-end**, when you'll spend an additional 2–3 days compiling the S01 Annual Return and generating P6 forms for each employee.

### Option 2: Accountant or Bookkeeper Handles It

Hiring an external accountant to process monthly payroll for a 5-person business in Jamaica typically costs:

- **J$15,000–J$35,000 per month** depending on complexity and the firm
- **J$180,000–J$420,000 per year**
- Add J$20,000–J$50,000 for year-end S01 and P6 preparation

**Total: J$200,000–J$470,000/year** for a 5-person payroll — and you're still dependent on someone else's schedule to pay your employees on time.

### Option 3: In-House Part-Time Help

A part-time bookkeeper or payroll assistant in Jamaica (even at 10 hours per week) costs:

- Minimum: J$50,000–J$80,000/month in salary
- Plus employer NIS, NHT, Education Tax (12.5% on top): ~J$8,000–J$10,000/month
- **Total: J$696,000–J$1,080,000/year** for part-time help

None of these options include the cost of errors. TAJ penalties for incorrect PAYE remittances start at 25% of the underpaid amount. A simple calculation mistake on the PAYE threshold can quietly accumulate into a significant liability by year-end.
