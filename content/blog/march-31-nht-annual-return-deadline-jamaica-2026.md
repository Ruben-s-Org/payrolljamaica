# March 31 NHT Annual Return Deadline: What Jamaican Employers Must Do Before April

**20 days to go.** The NHT annual return deadline — March 31, 2026 — is one of the most important statutory deadlines on the Jamaican payroll calendar. Every registered employer in Jamaica must file their annual NHT return (the P24 form) by this date, reporting total NHT contributions collected and remitted on behalf of employees during the 2025–2026 payroll year.

Missing this deadline triggers **late filing penalties** and can create complications for your employees when they apply for NHT benefits. Here is everything you need to know.

---

## What Is the NHT Annual Return (P24)?

The National Housing Trust (NHT) annual return — filed on the **P24 form** — is a year-end report that every Jamaican employer must submit. It summarises:

- **Total NHT contributions deducted** from employees during the year (2% of gross wages per employee)
- **Total employer NHT contributions** paid during the year (3% of gross wages per employee)
- **Contributions per employee** — so the NHT can update each worker's individual contribution record

The P24 is separate from the monthly S01 remittances you make throughout the year. The monthly remittances pay your NHT contributions on time; the P24 annual return reconciles the year's total and links contributions to individual employees.

---

## Who Must File the NHT Annual Return?

**Every employer registered with the NHT** must file the P24 annual return by March 31 each year. This includes:

- Companies and businesses with paid employees
- Sole traders with staff
- Non-profit organisations with employees
- Government agencies and statutory bodies (though their filing process differs)
- Employers whose NHT contributions were zero for the year (a nil return is still required)

If you registered your business with the NHT and have employed at least one person during the year, you must file.

---

## What Is the Penalty for Missing the March 31 Deadline?

Failing to file the NHT P24 annual return by March 31 attracts:

- **Late filing penalties** assessed by the NHT
- Possible **interest** on any contributions found to be outstanding
- Complications for **employees' NHT benefit applications** — workers may have gaps in their contribution records that delay mortgage benefit approvals

For employers with large employee counts, the late filing process is significantly more burdensome than filing on time. The NHT may require detailed reconciliation of monthly payments against the late return.

---

## What Do You Need to File the P24?

To complete your NHT P24 annual return, you need the following data for **every employee who was on your payroll during the year**:

| Required Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Employee NIS number | Unique identifier for NHT records |
| Employee name | Full legal name as registered |
| Total gross wages paid | Sum of all earnings for the year |
| Employee NHT deducted | 2% of gross wages |
| Employer NHT contributed | 3% of gross wages |
| Weeks/months employed | Duration of employment during the year |

For employees who joined or left mid-year, you report only the period they were employed by your company.

### Step 2: Reconcile with your monthly S01 payments

Verify that the total NHT contributions shown in your annual report match the total you remitted monthly via S01 throughout the year. Any discrepancies need to be investigated and resolved before filing.

### Step 3: Check employee NIS numbers

The NHT uses NIS numbers to match contributions to individual employee records. Verify that every active employee has a valid NIS number on file. Missing NIS numbers will cause the NHT to reject those contribution records, and your employee won't get credit for the contributions made on their behalf.

### Step 4: Include former employees

If any employee left your company during the year, they must still be included on the P24 for the period they worked for you. Do not omit departed employees from the annual return.

### Step 5: Submit by March 31

The P24 can be submitted:
- **Online** through the NHT employer portal
- **In person** at an NHT office
- **Via your registered payroll software** (Payroll Jamaica generates NHT-compliant export files)

## What About the SO1 Annual Return?

The **SO1** (also called the S01) is the **Tax Administration Jamaica (TAJ)** annual return — a separate filing from the NHT P24. The SO1 covers PAYE, NIS, and Education Tax and has its own annual deadline (typically also in March/April, though the exact date may differ from the NHT return).

If you are thinking about your March 31 deadline, confirm whether your obligation is the **NHT P24**, the **TAJ SO1**, or both. Many Jamaican employers have both filings due around the same time, making March the most critical month of the payroll year.

## Common Mistakes Jamaican Employers Make on the P24

### 1. Forgetting to include departed employees
Employees who left mid-year are still owed accurate NHT credit for the months they worked. Omitting them is a filing error.

### 2. Using gross pay without including all income types
The NHT contribution base includes **all remuneration** — basic salary, overtime, commission, bonuses, service charge (for hospitality workers), and allowances. Employers who only include base salary are under-reporting contributions.

### 3. Mismatched NIS numbers
A single digit error in an employee's NIS number means the NHT cannot match that contribution to the employee. This blocks the employee's benefit access and requires a correction filing.

### 4. Missing the deadline because you don't know who files it
In some businesses, the P24 falls between HR, Finance, and the external accountant — and nobody takes clear ownership. Assign one person to own the March 31 deadline now.

### 5. Waiting until March 28 to start
The P24 requires pulling a full year's payroll data and reconciling with monthly remittances. Starting the week before the deadline is too late if there are discrepancies to investigate.
