# Jamaica Employee Termination & Redundancy Pay: Complete Employer Guide 2026

Letting an employee go in Jamaica is one of the most legally consequential payroll events a business faces. Get the calculation wrong, skip a step, or miss a payment, and you face a labour tribunal claim — and labour disputes in Jamaica rarely resolve quickly or cheaply.

This guide covers everything Jamaican employers need to know about termination: the notice periods required by law, how redundancy pay is calculated under the Employment (Termination and Redundancy Payments) Act (ETRPA), what must appear on the final payslip, and the tax treatment of termination payments.

---

## The Two Types of Termination You Need to Know

### 1. Termination with Notice (Standard Dismissal)
The employer ends the employment relationship, giving the employee the legally required notice period (or payment in lieu of notice).

### 2. Redundancy (Retrenchment)
The position itself is being eliminated — not because of the employee's performance, but because of business restructuring, downsizing, or operational changes. Redundancy triggers specific statutory payments under the ETRPA in addition to notice.

The legal treatment differs significantly between these two situations. Misclassifying a redundancy as a standard dismissal to avoid redundancy payments is a common and expensive mistake.

---

## Minimum Notice Periods Under Jamaican Law

The Employment Act sets minimum notice periods based on how long the employee has worked for you:

| Length of Continuous Employment | Minimum Notice Required |
|---|---|
| Less than 2 years | 2 weeks |
| 2 years but less than 5 years | 4 weeks |
| 5 years but less than 10 years | 6 weeks |
| 10 years or more | 8 weeks |

**Payment in lieu of notice:** If you want the employee to leave immediately rather than work through the notice period, you must pay them their regular wages for the full notice period. This is called "payment in lieu of notice" and is treated as taxable emoluments for PAYE purposes.

**Contractual notice:** Many employment contracts specify longer notice periods than the statutory minimums. If your contract requires 3 months' notice but the Employment Act only requires 4 weeks, you must honour the contract.

---

## Redundancy Pay: Who Qualifies?

An employee qualifies for redundancy pay under the ETRPA if:
1. They have been **continuously employed for at least 2 years**
2. The termination is a **genuine redundancy** (the job is being eliminated)
3. They have not **resigned** or been dismissed for misconduct

Employees terminated for serious misconduct, gross negligence, or wilful disobedience are not entitled to redundancy pay — but the burden is on the employer to establish that the misconduct actually occurred and was serious enough to justify summary dismissal.

## How to Calculate Redundancy Pay in Jamaica

Redundancy pay under the ETRPA is calculated as follows:

**Formula:**
> **Redundancy Pay = 2 weeks' wages × number of complete years of service**

Where "2 weeks' wages" means the employee's average weekly wage over the 12 months immediately before the termination date, multiplied by 2.

### Example Calculation

**Scenario:** Maria has worked for your company for 7 years and 4 months. Her average weekly wage over the last 12 months was $35,000.

- Complete years of service: **7** (the 4 months do not count as a complete year)
- Two weeks' wages: $35,000 × 2 = **$70,000**
- Redundancy pay: $70,000 × 7 = **$490,000**

Maria would also receive payment in lieu of notice (6 weeks at $35,000 = $210,000) if she is not working out her notice period.

**Total separation payment:** $490,000 (redundancy) + $210,000 (notice) = $700,000

## Tax Treatment of Termination Payments

This is where many payroll teams make errors. Different components of a termination package have different tax treatment:

| Payment Component | PAYE Treatment | NIS | NHT | Education Tax |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Salary for notice period worked | Fully taxable | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Payment in lieu of notice | Fully taxable | No | No | Yes |
| Redundancy pay (ETRPA) | **Tax-exempt** | No | No | No |
| Ex gratia payments above ETRPA minimum | Taxable above J$500,000 exemption | No | No | No |

**Key point on redundancy pay:** Statutory redundancy pay calculated under the ETRPA is **exempt from income tax**. You should not withhold PAYE from the redundancy pay component. This is frequently mishandled by employers who lump all termination payments together and apply PAYE across the board.

**The $500,000 exemption for ex-gratia payments:** If you pay more than the statutory ETRPA minimum as a goodwill gesture (ex-gratia payment), the first $500,000 of any such payment is tax-exempt. Amounts above $500,000 are taxable.

## Final Payslip Requirements

The final payslip at termination must include:

1. **All outstanding regular wages** up to the termination date
2. **Accrued vacation pay** — any unused vacation time must be paid out
3. **Payment in lieu of notice** (if applicable)
4. **Redundancy pay** (if a genuine redundancy)
5. **All statutory deductions** correctly applied to the taxable portions
6. **NIS, NHT, Education Tax** withheld on the applicable taxable amounts
7. **A breakdown** showing each component clearly

The employee must receive their final payslip on or before their last day of employment, or within a reasonable time thereafter.

## What Counts as "Continuous Employment"?

Breaks in employment can complicate the calculation. In Jamaica, the following generally do **not** break continuity of employment:

- Sick leave (paid or unpaid)
- Maternity leave
- Annual leave
- Short-term temporary layoffs where re-employment was expected
- Transfer of the business to a new owner (the new employer inherits the employee's tenure)

Intentional gaps created to avoid triggering the 2-year threshold — for example, dismissing a worker at 23 months and re-hiring immediately — are viewed as abuse of the Act and may still give rise to ETRPA claims.

---

## Business Transfer: A Common Trap

If you sell your business, merge, or restructure in a way that transfers employees to a new entity, the **new employer inherits each employee's continuous service history**. The clock does not reset.

This means an employee who has worked for the original business for 8 years and is retained by the purchaser has 8 years' service from day one with the new owner — relevant for future redundancy calculations and notice periods.

## Documenting the Redundancy

A redundancy without proper documentation is a liability. At minimum:

1. **Written notice** to the affected employee specifying:
   - The date of termination
   - That the termination is by reason of redundancy
   - The redundancy pay amount they will receive
   - The notice period (or that payment in lieu will be made)

2. **Selection criteria records** — if some employees in similar roles were retained and others were made redundant, you need to document how selections were made (and ensure the criteria are not discriminatory).

3. **Consultation** — for large-scale redundancies (20 or more employees), the Labour Relations and Industrial Disputes Act requires consultation with affected employees and potentially the labour union if one is recognised.

4. **Labour Ministry notification** — for mass redundancies, employers must notify the Ministry of Labour.

---

## Severance Pay vs. Redundancy Pay: A Clarification

These terms are often used interchangeably in Jamaica, but technically:

- **Redundancy pay** is the statutory entitlement under the ETRPA — calculated using the 2 weeks × years formula.
- **Severance pay** is sometimes used to describe the total separation package including notice pay, ex-gratia payments, and other benefits.

When an employee asks about their severance entitlement, they usually mean the full package. Make sure you are clear about which components you are discussing.

## Quick Reference: Termination Checklist

- [ ] Determine the correct notice period based on years of service
- [ ] Decide: working notice period or payment in lieu
- [ ] Confirm whether the termination is a redundancy (ETRPA applies)
- [ ] Calculate redundancy pay: (2 weeks' average wages) × (complete years of service)
- [ ] Calculate payment in lieu of notice (if not working out notice)
- [ ] Calculate accrued vacation pay
- [ ] Apply correct PAYE treatment (redundancy pay is tax-exempt)
- [ ] Prepare final payslip showing all components
- [ ] Deliver written termination letter documenting the reason
- [ ] Issue P24 certificate by March 31 of the following tax year
