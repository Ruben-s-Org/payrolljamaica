## What Happens on April 1, 2026?

Jamaica's fiscal year runs from April 1 to March 31. That means April 1 marks the official start of tax year 2026/2027. Every statutory deduction clock resets. NIS contribution ceilings refresh. Your cumulative PAYE calculations start from zero. If you have been limping through year-end on spreadsheets, this is the moment you either fix the process — or carry the chaos forward into a brand-new year.

The Tax Administration Jamaica (TAJ) requires employers to file their monthly S01 returns by the 14th of the following month. That means your **first S01 return for the new tax year is due May 14, 2026**, covering April's payroll. Missing it comes with interest and penalties. There is no grace period for "we were still setting things up."

## The Current Statutory Rates (2026/2027)

Before you run a single payslip, confirm your payroll system is configured with the correct deduction rates. Here is the full picture:

| Deduction | Employee | Employer |
|---|---|---|
| PAYE | Graduated (threshold: J$125,008/month) | — |
| NIS | 3% | 3% |
| NHT | 2% | 3% |
| Education Tax | 2.25% | 3.5% |
| HEART | — | 3% |

**PAYE threshold:** J$1,500,096 per year, or J$125,008 per month. Employees earning below this threshold pay no income tax — but all other statutory deductions still apply regardless of earnings.

If these numbers are not locked into your payroll system correctly from April 1, every payslip you run is potentially wrong. And wrong payslips become wrong S01 filings, which become TAJ notices.

## Your New Tax Year Employer Checklist

Use this before you run your first April payroll:

- [ ] Confirm all employee records are current — name, TRN, NIS number, NHT number
- [ ] Reset or verify cumulative year-to-date figures are at zero
- [ ] Confirm PAYE threshold is set to J$125,008/month
- [ ] Verify NIS, NHT, Education Tax, and HEART rates are correctly entered
- [ ] Check that your NIS ceiling (~J$5M annually) is properly configured
- [ ] Update any salary changes effective April 1
- [ ] Confirm your pay frequency (weekly, fortnightly, monthly) aligns with your S01 filing
- [ ] Set a calendar reminder for May 14, 2026 — your first S01 due date
- [ ] Run a test calculation before processing live payroll

Eight items. Every one of them matters.
