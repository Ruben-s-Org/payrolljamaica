# Jamaica Income Tax Threshold 2026/27 — What Every Employer Needs to Know

The Jamaica income tax threshold is the annual salary amount below which employees pay **zero PAYE (Pay-As-You-Earn) income tax**. For the 2026/27 tax year (starting April 1, 2026), the PAYE-free threshold remains **JMD $1,500,096 per year** — equivalent to **JMD $125,008 per month** or **JMD $28,848 per week**.

Every Jamaican employer must apply this threshold correctly to every payroll calculation. Get it wrong and you either over-deduct PAYE (reducing employees' take-home pay illegally) or under-deduct (creating a tax liability).

---

## 2026/27 Jamaica Income Tax Threshold at a Glance

| Period | Tax-Free Amount |
|--------|----------------|
| Annual | JMD $1,500,096 |
| Monthly | JMD $125,008 |
| Fortnightly | JMD $57,696 |
| Weekly | JMD $28,848 |
| Daily (260 working days) | JMD $5,770 |

Employees earning below these amounts in each pay period pay **zero PAYE income tax**.

## How the Income Tax Threshold Works in Jamaica

The threshold is a personal allowance — it reduces the taxable income before PAYE rates are applied. Jamaica's PAYE system works as follows:

### Taxable Income Calculation
```
Gross Pay
− Income Tax Threshold (JMD $125,008/month)
= Taxable Income
```

### PAYE Rates Applied to Taxable Income
| Taxable Income (Annual) | PAYE Rate |
|-------------------------|-----------|
| JMD $0 — $0 (below threshold) | 0% |
| JMD $0 — $6,000,000 (above threshold) | 25% |
| Above JMD $6,000,000 | 30% |

So for an employee earning JMD $200,000/month:
- Taxable income = JMD $200,000 − JMD $125,008 = **JMD $74,992**
- PAYE = 25% × JMD $74,992 = **JMD $18,748/month**

For an employee earning JMD $100,000/month:
- Taxable income = JMD $100,000 − JMD $125,008 = **JMD −$25,008** (below threshold)
- PAYE = **JMD $0** (no tax deducted)

## Has the Threshold Changed for 2026/27?

**No change has been announced for 2026/27.** The JMD $1,500,096 annual threshold was set during the 2021/22 budget and has remained unchanged. There was speculation about an increase before the April 2026 budget presentation, but no adjustment was enacted.

This is significant because with inflation, the *real value* of the threshold has eroded — meaning more employees now fall into the taxable range than when the threshold was first set.

**Employer action:** Your payroll system should already be using JMD $1,500,096. If you are still using an older threshold figure, update it immediately. Payroll Jamaica automatically applies the correct 2026/27 threshold on April 1, 2026.

## Other Statutory Deductions for April 2026

The income tax threshold only affects PAYE. Other statutory deductions have their own rules:

### NIS (National Insurance Scheme) — April 2026
- **Employee contribution:** 3% of gross earnings
- **Employer contribution:** 3% of gross earnings
- **Annual ceiling:** JMD $5,000,000 (contributions stop once employee reaches this annual gross)
- **No income threshold** — NIS applies from the first dollar earned

### NHT (National Housing Trust) — April 2026
- **Employee contribution:** 2% of gross earnings
- **Employer contribution:** 3% of gross earnings
- **No annual ceiling** — NHT applies on all earnings with no cap

### Education Tax — April 2026
- **Employee contribution:** 2.25% of gross earnings
- **Employer contribution:** 3.5% of gross earnings
- **No annual ceiling** — Education Tax applies on all earnings

## Common Mistakes with the Jamaica Income Tax Threshold

### Mistake 1: Applying the annual threshold to a monthly payroll without dividing
Some employers enter JMD $1,500,096 as the monthly threshold. The correct monthly equivalent is **JMD $125,008**.

### Mistake 2: Not applying the threshold pro-rata for partial months
If an employee starts work on March 15 and receives half a month's pay, the threshold should also be half: JMD $62,504. Do not deduct PAYE from below-threshold earnings.

### Mistake 3: Confusing the threshold with NIS/NHT ceilings
The income tax threshold only applies to PAYE. NIS has an annual *ceiling* (contributions stop at JMD $5M gross). NHT has no ceiling. These are different mechanisms.

### Mistake 4: Forgetting to reset at the new tax year
Carry-over of prior year deductions into the new tax year is a common source of PAYE over-deductions. Reset all year-to-date accumulators on April 1.

## How to Check if Your Payroll is Calculating PAYE Correctly

**Manual verification:**
1. Take the employee's monthly gross pay
2. Subtract JMD $125,008 (2026 monthly threshold)
3. If the result is negative or zero, PAYE = JMD $0
4. If positive, multiply by 25% (or 30% if annual taxable income exceeds JMD $6M)

**Example:**
- Employee gross: JMD $300,000/month
- Less threshold: JMD $300,000 − JMD $125,008 = JMD $174,992
- PAYE: 25% × JMD $174,992 = **JMD $43,748/month**

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the income tax threshold in Jamaica for 2026?**
The annual income tax threshold (PAYE-free amount) is JMD $1,500,096, equivalent to JMD $125,008/month.

**Q: Has the Jamaica tax-free threshold changed for the new tax year 2026/27?**
No. The JMD $1,500,096 threshold has not changed for 2026/27. It has been unchanged since 2021/22.

**Q: Does the income tax threshold apply to NIS and NHT?**
No. The income tax threshold only applies to PAYE (income tax). NIS has a separate annual contribution ceiling (JMD $5M). NHT and Education Tax have no threshold or ceiling.

**Q: What happens if I deduct too much PAYE from an employee?**
The employer is required to issue a P24 form showing deductions. The employee can then claim a refund from TAJ when filing their annual income tax return.

**Q: At what salary does PAYE start in Jamaica?**
PAYE starts on any monthly salary above JMD $125,008 (or annual salary above JMD $1,500,096).
