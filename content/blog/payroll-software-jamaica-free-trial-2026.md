## Why Free Trials Matter More for Jamaica Payroll Software

In most software categories, a free trial is about testing usability. For Jamaica payroll software, the stakes are higher.

**Jamaica-specific compliance cannot be assumed.** Most payroll software sold internationally is built for the UK, US, or Canada market and adapted for Jamaica as an afterthought. The tax logic may be partially correct — PAYE bands might be configured, but the NHT employer contribution split might be wrong, or the NIS contribution ceiling might not be applied correctly. You will not discover this from a product demo. You will discover it when an employee challenges their payslip or TAJ flags a discrepancy.

**Errors compound over time.** If your payroll software is calculating PAYE incorrectly from January, by December you have twelve months of errors to unwind. A free trial lets you run your actual payroll numbers through the system and verify the outputs against what you know to be correct before you go live.

**Setup time is real.** Migrating employee data, configuring pay schedules, and setting up statutory deduction profiles takes time. A proper trial period lets you complete this setup without financial commitment — so if the software does not fit, you have not wasted a month's subscription fee discovering it.

## What a Proper Jamaica Payroll Free Trial Should Include

Not all free trials are created equal. Watch for these signs that a trial is genuinely useful versus a limited demo designed to frustrate you into upgrading.

### Full access to payroll calculations

The core functionality — PAYE calculation, NIS, NHT, and Education Tax deductions — should be fully accessible during the trial. A trial that lets you add employees but restricts payroll runs is not a payroll trial. It is a contacts database with a paywall.

### Ability to run actual payroll

You should be able to process a complete payroll cycle — enter employee gross pay, see all deductions calculated, and generate payslips — during the trial. Run your real numbers for at least one pay period and verify the outputs manually. If the trial prevents you from doing this, it is hiding something.

### Payslip and report generation

Jamaica employers are legally required to provide payslips. A trial should let you generate and review payslip output so you can confirm the format is compliant and professional before committing.

### No credit card required to start

A trial that requires payment details upfront creates friction and trust issues. A vendor confident in their product does not need your card details before you have even run your first payroll.

### Enough time to actually test it

A 7-day trial is rarely sufficient for payroll software. You need time to set up employees, run a payroll cycle, check the outputs, and ask support questions if something does not look right. Look for 14 days minimum.
