# Jamaica Payroll Compliance Checklist: Everything Due Before March 31, 2026

March 31 is 19 days away. For Jamaican employers, it is the single most important deadline of the year — the close of the 2025/26 tax year triggers a cascade of filings, reconciliations, and submissions that every employer must complete.

Miss a deadline and you're looking at TAJ penalties, NHT late fees (20% per annum), and a growing backlog that becomes harder to clear every day you delay.

Use this checklist to make sure nothing slips through.

---

## The March 31 Deadline Stack

Here is every obligation that lands on or around March 31 for Jamaican employers:

| Obligation | Deadline | Who You File With |
|---|---|---|
| S01 Annual Employer Return | March 31 | Tax Administration Jamaica (TAJ) |
| NHT Annual Return | March 31 | National Housing Trust |
| Final PAYE monthly remittance (March run) | April 14 | TAJ |
| Final NHT monthly contribution (March run) | April 14 | NHT |
| Final NIS contribution (March run) | April 14 | NIS |
| Final Education Tax (March run) | April 14 | TAJ |
| P24 Annual Return (employer totals by employee) | April 30 | TAJ |

**Note:** The March payroll run itself happens in March (or on April 1 if you pay first-of-month), but the March remittances aren't due until April 14. The March 31 hard deadlines are the **annual returns** — the S01 and NHT returns that summarise the entire April 2025–March 2026 year.

## Pre-March 31 Checklist

Work through this in order. Each item depends on the ones before it.

### Step 1: Gather Full-Year Payroll Records ☐

Before you can file any annual return, you need accurate records for every pay period from April 2025 to March 2026.

- [ ] Pull payroll records for all 12 months (April 2025 – March 2026)
- [ ] Confirm you have monthly totals for: gross wages, PAYE withheld, NHT contributed (employer + employee), NIS contributed (employer + employee), Education Tax
- [ ] Identify any months where filing was missed or payment was late — you'll need to address these before filing the annual return

**If you've been using payroll software:** This data is all in your dashboard. Export a full-year summary report.

**If you've been running payroll manually:** This step may take a day or more. Do it now.

---

### Step 2: Reconcile Each Employee's Records ☐

The S01 and NHT annual returns require per-employee breakdowns, not just totals.

For each active employee (and any employees who left during the year):

- [ ] Total gross wages paid: April 2025 – March 2026
- [ ] Total PAYE withheld
- [ ] Total NHT contributions (employee + employer, per NHT number)
- [ ] Total NIS contributions (verify the annual cap of J$5,000,000 was applied correctly)
- [ ] Total Education Tax (employee + employer)
- [ ] Confirm NHT numbers are recorded correctly for all employees

**Common reconciliation errors:**
- NIS deductions continued past the J$5,000,000 cap for high earners
- NHT contributions remitted without NHT numbers (contributions not credited to employees)
- Education Tax omitted entirely for some or all months
- New employees added mid-year without PAYE being calculated cumulatively from their start date

---

### Step 3: File the S01 Annual Employer Return ☐

**Deadline: March 31, 2026**

The S01 Annual Return summarises your full payroll year — total remuneration paid, PAYE withheld, Education Tax collected, NHT and NIS contributions — by employee and in aggregate.

**How to file:**
1. Log into TAJ e-Services at [jamaicatax.gov.jm](https://www.jamaicatax.gov.jm)
2. Navigate to Employer Returns → S01 Annual Return
3. Enter the April 2025 – March 2026 totals per employee
4. Confirm and submit

**If you have outstanding monthly S01 returns:** You cannot file the annual return cleanly until monthly returns are current. File any missing monthly returns first.

**If you have outstanding PAYE balances:** Pay these before or simultaneously with the annual return filing to avoid additional interest accumulation.

---

### Step 4: File the NHT Annual Return ☐

**Deadline: March 31, 2026**

The NHT Annual Return is a separate filing from the TAJ S01. It reports every employee's NHT contributions for the year, tied to their individual NHT numbers.

**How to file:**
1. Log into the NHT employer portal at [nhts.gov.jm](https://www.nhts.gov.jm)
2. Enter annual NHT contributions per employee
3. Verify each employee's NHT number is correctly recorded
4. Submit the annual return

**Late filing penalty: 20% per annum on outstanding NHT balances.** This compounds quickly — file by March 31.

---

### Step 5: Issue P45 Forms to Departed Employees ☐

If any employees left your employment during the 2025/26 tax year, you must have issued (or must issue now) a P45 — a certificate showing their year-to-date earnings and PAYE deducted at time of departure.

- [ ] List all employees who left between April 2025 and March 2026
- [ ] Confirm P45 was issued within 30 days of their departure
- [ ] If any were missed, issue them now

---

### Step 6: Prepare for March Payroll Run ☐

Your final payroll run of the 2025/26 tax year covers March 2026 earnings. For most businesses, this run happens in late March or on April 1.

- [ ] Run March payroll with updated year-to-date PAYE calculations (this is the final cumulative PAYE check for the year)
- [ ] Generate payslips for all March employees
- [ ] Prepare S01 monthly return for March (due April 14)
- [ ] Prepare NHT and NIS submissions for March (due April 14)

---

### Step 7: Plan for P24 Filing ☐

**Deadline: April 30, 2026**

The P24 is the annual employer declaration filed with the TAJ — it summarises total remuneration and PAYE withheld per employee for the full year. Unlike the S01 Annual Return, it has an April 30 deadline.

- [ ] Confirm your full-year per-employee records are complete and reconciled
- [ ] Note April 30 in your calendar — do not mistake this for a March 31 deadline

---

## What Happens If You Miss March 31

**TAJ (S01 Annual Return):**
Interest accrues at 16.62% per annum on any outstanding PAYE, plus administrative penalties for late filing. The TAJ has been increasing enforcement of annual return deadlines.

**NHT (Annual Return):**
20% per annum penalty on any outstanding NHT contributions. The NHT actively pursues employers with outstanding contributions — don't assume it will go unnoticed.

**Compounding effect:**
Every month you delay makes the reconciliation more complex and the penalties larger. A March 31 miss becomes a June problem that's 3× harder to fix.
