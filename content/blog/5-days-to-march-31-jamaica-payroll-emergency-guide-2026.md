# 5 Days to March 31: Jamaica Payroll Emergency Guide — Act Now or Pay Penalties

If you are reading this with 5 days left before Jamaica's March 31 payroll deadline and you have not yet filed your SO1 Annual Return, NHT Annual Return, or NIS Reconciliation — you are not alone. And you are not out of time. But you must act today.

Five days is enough time to file on time if you start right now. It is not enough time if you spend two of those days gathering records that should have been organized weeks ago. This guide tells you exactly what to do, in what order, to get across the line by March 31.

---

## What Happens if You Miss March 31

TAJ penalties begin on April 1 — automatically, with no warning, no grace period.

| Agency | Penalty Structure |
|---|---|
| TAJ (PAYE) | 10% late penalty + 20% surcharge + 2% monthly interest |
| NHT | 20% surcharge — automatic, no appeal |
| NIS | Surcharge + potential court action for repeat offenders |

On a business with J$300,000 annual PAYE liability, missing the deadline costs J$90,000 in first-year penalties alone. That number compounds every month you remain unfiled.

There is no negotiating the start date of these penalties. Filing on April 1 is late. The only way to avoid them is to file by March 31.

## Emergency Triage: What Is Your Situation?

**Situation A: All monthly S02 remittances are up to date, just haven't done the annual return yet**
This is the easiest fix. Your data is reconciled — you just need to generate and submit the three annual returns. You can do this today. Start with the SO1 via eTAJ, then NHT Annual Return via the NHT employer portal, then NIS reconciliation.

**Situation B: Some monthly S02s are missing, plus no annual return**
This is more complex. You cannot file an accurate SO1 that doesn't reconcile with what you actually remitted monthly. Calculate arrears first, remit catch-up payments, then file the annual return. Initiate the catch-up remittances today — bank processing can take 1–2 business days.

**Situation C: You've been doing payroll manually and don't have organized records**
This is the hardest situation. You need to reconstruct your 2025 payroll from available sources: salary agreements, bank transfer records, WhatsApp payment confirmations, cash payment logs. Even a rough reconstruction is better than no filing. A partial, good-faith filing with an explanation gets treated differently by TAJ than complete non-compliance.

**Situation D: You've never filed before (new employer or informal compliance)**
Contact a TAJ office today. Explain your situation. First-time compliance conversations are treated differently from repeat non-compliance. Do not avoid the conversation — TAJ engagement almost always results in a better outcome than non-engagement.

## Your 5-Day Emergency Plan

### Day 1 (Today) — Gather and Assess
Block 3 hours. Collect:
- Your payroll register or salary records for the full 2025 tax year (April 2025 – March 2026)
- S02 remittance receipts for all 12 months
- A list of all employees who were on payroll at any point during 2025, including terminated employees

Compare your S02 receipts to your payroll register. Identify gaps. Quantify any arrears. This assessment is non-negotiable — you cannot file without knowing what you've already paid.

### Day 2 — Fix the Gaps
If you have missing S02 months, calculate and remit the outstanding amounts today. Use the [PayrollJamaica calculator](https://payrolljamaica.com/calculator) to verify the correct amounts if your records are uncertain. Initiate bank transfers or pay at a TAJ office today so the payments process before March 31.

### Day 3 — Prepare the Three Returns

**SO1 Annual Return:**
- Log into eTAJ (etaj.gov.jm)
- Select SO1 Annual Return
- Enter each employee's full-year figures: gross pay, PAYE, NHT (employee + employer), NIS (employee + employer), Education Tax (employee + employer), HEART
- Verify totals match your S02 submissions

**NHT Annual Return:**
- Log into the NHT employer portal (nht.gov.jm)
- Complete the annual return with full-year contribution figures per employee

**NIS Reconciliation:**
- Prepare your reconciliation form for the National Insurance Scheme
- This is submitted to the Ministry of Labour and Social Security — not through eTAJ

### Day 4 — Submit Everything
Submit all three returns. Do not wait for Day 5. Today's submittals are tomorrow's confirmed receipts. eTAJ portal traffic is heaviest on March 30–31. If you submit on Day 4, you have a full day to resolve any portal issues before the deadline.

Save every confirmation receipt. Screenshot every submission acknowledgement. These are your compliance records.

### Day 5 (March 30) — Verify and Buffer
Log back into eTAJ, the NHT portal, and confirm your NIS submission is received. Verify all submissions show "Filed" or "Received" — not "Pending." If anything is still pending, resolve it today. Do not assume it will clear by midnight.

## Rate Reference for Emergency Calculations

If your records have gaps, use these rates to reconstruct correct figures:

| Deduction | Employee Rate | Employer Rate |
|---|---|---|
| NIS | 3% | 3% |
| NHT | 2% | 3% |
| Education Tax | 2.25% | 3.5% |
| HEART/NSTA | — | 3% |
| PAYE | Progressive (0% below J$1,500,096/year) | N/A |

**PAYE income tax threshold: J$1,500,096 per year (J$125,008 per month)**. Employees earning below this have no PAYE liability. Above it, the standard income tax rate applies to the excess.
