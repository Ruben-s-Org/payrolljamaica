# Payroll Software Jamaica: Cost, Pricing, and What You Actually Get in 2026

If you're a Jamaican business owner evaluating payroll software, the first question is usually: *how much does it actually cost?* The second is usually: *and what do I get for that price?*

The answers matter more than you'd think. Payroll software is one of those categories where the sticker price rarely tells the whole story. Hidden fees, charges for statutory filings, year-end surcharges, and per-seat pricing for HR modules can easily double what you expected to pay.

This guide breaks down what Jamaica-specific payroll software costs in 2026, what to watch out for, and how to calculate the real per-employee cost for your business.

---

## What Does Payroll Software in Jamaica Actually Cost?

Payroll software for Jamaican businesses typically falls into one of three pricing models:

### 1. Per-Employee Per-Month Pricing (Most Common)

You pay a fixed rate for each employee on your payroll each month. This is the most transparent model and the easiest to budget for.

**Typical range in Jamaica:** J$1,500 – J$5,000 per employee per month

What usually determines where you land in that range:
- **Number of employees** — most software gives volume discounts
- **Level of automation** — basic calculation vs. full S01 filing support
- **Support level** — self-serve vs. dedicated account manager
- **Payslip delivery** — printed vs. digital self-service portal

**PayrollJamaica pricing:** From J$2,500 per employee per month — includes all statutory calculations (PAYE, NHT, NIS, Education Tax), payslip generation, and S01 return exports. No extra charge for year-end returns or adding employees mid-month.

### 2. Flat Monthly Fee (Less Common, Usually for Larger Businesses)

Some providers charge a flat monthly fee regardless of headcount. This can be cost-effective at scale but rarely makes sense for smaller teams.

**Typical range:** J$25,000 – J$150,000/month flat

This model is more common with managed payroll bureaus rather than software platforms.

### 3. Per-Payrun Pricing

Some older platforms charge per payroll run rather than per employee. This works if you run infrequent payroll but becomes expensive for weekly or bi-weekly payroll schedules.

## What Should Payroll Software in Jamaica Include at Any Price Point?

Before comparing costs, make sure you're comparing like with like. Jamaica-specific payroll software should include:

### Non-Negotiables (Should Always Be Included)
- **PAYE calculation** using current TAJ thresholds (2026: J$1,500,096/year, 25%/30%)
- **NHT contribution tracking** — employee 2%, employer 3%
- **NIS calculation** with maximum insurable wage cap enforcement
- **Education Tax calculation** — 2.25% employee, 3.5% employer
- **Payslip generation** in a format that satisfies Jamaican employment requirements
- **S01 return export** — ready to upload to TAJ e-Services
- **NHT return generation**
- **Rate updates** when Jamaica's statutory rates change

### Should Be Included (Watch Out If They're Extras)
- **Year-end P24 and annual S01 return generation** — some providers charge extra for this
- **Adding employees mid-month** — some legacy platforms charge per-change fees
- **Multi-user access** — basic access for an accountant or HR manager should be included
- **Data export** — your own payroll data shouldn't be paywalled

### Optional / Premium Add-Ons
- **Employee self-service portal** — employees access their own payslips online
- **Leave management** — track vacation, sick leave, maternity leave
- **Direct deposit file generation** for bank payroll uploads (NCB, Scotiabank, etc.)
- **Time and attendance integration**
- **Accounting software sync** (QuickBooks, Xero, Sage)

## Hidden Costs to Watch For

Several Jamaica payroll software providers (particularly regional Caribbean platforms not built specifically for Jamaica) have pricing structures that look attractive until you read the fine print.

### Common Hidden Fees

**Statutory filing fees:** Some providers charge separately per S01 submission. At J$2,000–J$5,000 per filing × 12 months = J$24,000–J$60,000/year that isn't in the base price.

**Year-end report fees:** P24, annual NHT return, and year-end reconciliation are sometimes charged as a separate "year-end service fee" of J$10,000–J$50,000+.

**Setup/onboarding fees:** One-time setup fees of J$15,000–J$50,000 are common with managed payroll bureaus. Software platforms should generally not charge these.

**Per-payslip printing fees:** If you have employees who need physical payslips, some providers charge per printed slip.

**Support fees:** Basic email support is usually included, but phone or priority support is sometimes an add-on.

**Data export fees:** Some platforms make it expensive to leave by charging to export your historical payroll data.

### How to Evaluate Total Cost — Example Calculation

**Scenario:** 10 employees, monthly payroll, basic PAYE/NHT/NIS/Education Tax, digital payslips

## Payroll Software vs. Managed Payroll Bureau: Which Is Right for You?

Many Jamaican businesses face a choice between:

**Payroll software** (you or your HR/finance team runs payroll each month)
- Lower cost: J$1,500–J$5,000/employee/month
- You control the timeline and process
- Good for businesses with at least one person comfortable with numbers
- Best for: 5–200 employee businesses with internal HR or finance capacity

**Managed payroll bureau** (a firm handles your payroll end-to-end)
- Higher cost: J$5,000–J$15,000+/employee/month (or flat fees)
- No internal effort required — they handle everything
- Good for businesses that have zero bandwidth for payroll admin
- Best for: very small businesses (under 5 employees) or large businesses wanting full outsourcing

For most Jamaican SMEs in the 5–100 employee range, payroll software with good Jamaica-specific support hits the sweet spot.

## ROI of Payroll Software for Jamaican Businesses

The case for payroll software goes beyond the direct cost comparison. Consider what you're actually buying:

**Time saved:**
Manual payroll for 10 employees in Jamaica (calculating PAYE, NHT, NIS, Education Tax, generating payslips, preparing S01) takes 4–8 hours per month. At J$2,500/hour for a skilled bookkeeper, that's J$10,000–J$20,000/month in labour cost alone — more than the software typically costs.

**Penalty avoidance:**
A single incorrect NHT return can result in a 20% annual penalty on outstanding contributions. For a business with a J$500,000/month payroll, a 10% NHT discrepancy would cost J$50,000/year in penalties. The software pays for itself on the first penalty it prevents.

**Compliance confidence:**
When the TAJ, NHT, or NIS contacts you for an audit or query, having clean, software-generated payroll records dramatically simplifies your response and reduces exposure.
