# 11 Days to March 31: Payroll Reconciliation Guide for Jamaican Employers

You have 11 days. The filing deadline is not a suggestion. March 31 is fixed, TAJ and NHT systems will be overwhelmed in the final days, and if your numbers do not reconcile, you cannot file with confidence.

Payroll reconciliation is the part that catches most Jamaican employers off guard. They think their payroll has been running fine all year — and then they try to add up 12 months of numbers and discover that nothing quite matches.

If your PAYE totals do not agree with your S01 filings, if your NHT annual return numbers do not match your monthly schedules, or if your NIS contributions are off by even a few hundred dollars — you have a reconciliation problem that must be solved before March 31.

Here is how to do it systematically, with 11 days on the clock.

---

## What Does "Payroll Reconciliation" Actually Mean?

Payroll reconciliation is the process of verifying that three sets of numbers agree:

1. **What you calculated** — the payroll deductions shown on employee payslips each month
2. **What you filed** — the amounts reported on S01 returns (TAJ), NHT schedules, and NIS schedules
3. **What you paid** — the actual remittances made to TAJ, NHT, and NIS via bank payments

If all three match for every statutory deduction across all 12 months, congratulations — your year-end filing is straightforward.

If they do not match — and for most employers running manual payroll, they will not match perfectly — you need to identify and resolve every discrepancy before you file.

## The Four Reconciliation Areas

### 1. PAYE Reconciliation

PAYE is usually the largest statutory deduction and the most common source of errors.

**What to reconcile:**
- Total PAYE deducted from all employee payslips (April 2025–March 2026)
- Total PAYE reported across all 12 monthly S01 returns
- Total PAYE remitted to TAJ (bank payment records)

**Common discrepancies:**

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Payslip total > S01 total | PAYE calculated but not reported on the S01 | File amended S01 for affected month(s) |
| S01 total > payslip total | Over-reported on S01 (data entry error) | File correction with TAJ |
| S01 total > bank payments | Filed the return but did not remit full amount | Pay outstanding balance + interest before March 31 |
| Threshold error | Used wrong PAYE threshold (correct: J$125,008/month or J$1,500,096/year) | Recalculate affected months |

Use the [PayrollJamaica calculator](https://payrolljamaica.com/calculator) to verify PAYE calculations for any employee salary. Enter the gross pay and confirm the PAYE amount matches what you deducted.

### 2. NIS Reconciliation

NIS contributions have a ceiling — J$5,000,000 per year (approximately J$416,667 per month). Employees earning above the ceiling should not have NIS deducted on the excess.

**What to reconcile:**
- Total NIS employee deductions (3% of gross, capped at ceiling)
- Total NIS employer contributions (3% of gross, capped at ceiling)
- NIS amounts reported on monthly schedules
- NIS amounts actually remitted

**Common problems:**
- Employee hit the annual ceiling mid-year but deductions continued
- New employee's NIS number was entered incorrectly on schedules
- Part-time employee paid below the NIS minimum wage threshold

### 3. NHT Reconciliation

NHT has no ceiling — 2% employee and 3% employer on all gross emoluments, regardless of amount.

**What to reconcile:**
- Total NHT contributions per employee (for the annual return)
- Sum of all monthly NHT schedule filings
- Actual NHT remittances made

**Key check:** Your NHT annual return total must equal the sum of 12 monthly NHT schedule totals. If it does not, the NHT will flag your return.

### 4. Education Tax Reconciliation

Education Tax rates — 2.25% employee, 3.5% employer — apply to gross emoluments with no ceiling.

**What to reconcile:**
- Total Education Tax deducted from payslips
- Total Education Tax reported on S01 returns
- Total Education Tax remitted

Education Tax is reported on the same S01 as PAYE, so errors here usually show up together with PAYE discrepancies.

## The Reconciliation Process: Step by Step

### Step 1: Build Your Master Spreadsheet

Create a reconciliation worksheet with these columns for each month (April 2025 through March 2026):

| Month | Gross Payroll | PAYE Deducted | PAYE on S01 | PAYE Remitted | NIS Emp | NIS Empr | NHT Emp | NHT Empr | EdTax Emp | EdTax Empr |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Fill in every cell from your payroll records, filed returns, and bank statements. Missing data in any cell is a red flag that needs investigation.

### Step 2: Calculate Variances

For each month and each deduction type, calculate:
- **Variance A:** Payslip amount minus filed amount (calculation vs. reporting)
- **Variance B:** Filed amount minus remitted amount (reporting vs. payment)

Any non-zero variance needs an explanation. Acceptable explanations include:
- Rounding (should be less than J$50 per month per deduction type)
- Timing (payment made in the next month — but was it on time?)
- Known adjustment (documented correction with TAJ or NHT)

Unacceptable: "I don't know why these don't match." That means there is an error somewhere and you need to find it.

### Step 3: Cross-Check Employee-Level Data

After reconciling at the company level, spot-check individual employees:

**High earners** — verify PAYE was calculated correctly using the 25% rate up to J$6,000,000 and 30% above. Verify NIS contributions stopped at the ceiling.

**New hires** — verify their start month is correct. A common error: employee started in June but first appeared on payroll in July, meaning June contributions are missing.

**Leavers** — verify their final month is correct and that no contributions were deducted after their last working day.

**Bonus recipients** — verify bonus months had higher statutory deductions. Bonuses attract PAYE, NIS (if below ceiling), NHT, and Education Tax.

### Step 4: Resolve Discrepancies

For each discrepancy found:

1. **Determine the correct amount** — recalculate using the correct rates and the [PayrollJamaica calculator](https://payrolljamaica.com/calculator)
2. **Identify which record is wrong** — payslip, filed return, or remittance
3. **Correct the record:**
   - Wrong payslip → adjust employee records (may need to issue corrected P6)
   - Wrong S01 → file amended return with TAJ
   - Short remittance → pay the difference before March 31 to minimise penalties
   - Over-remittance → claim credit on next filing period

### Step 5: Document Everything

Keep a reconciliation log showing:
- Each discrepancy found
- Root cause
- Corrective action taken
- Date resolved

If TAJ or NHT ever audits you, this log is your defence. It shows you identified and corrected problems proactively — which is treated very differently from errors discovered during an audit.

## Red Flags That Mean You Need Professional Help

Some reconciliation problems are beyond a simple fix. Get your accountant or payroll advisor involved immediately if:

- **Total discrepancy exceeds J$100,000** in any single deduction type
- **Multiple months have the same systematic error** (suggests the wrong rate was applied all year)
- **You cannot locate one or more monthly S01 returns** (may not have been filed)
- **Bank records show missed remittance months** (late payment penalties and interest apply)
- **Employee NIS or NHT numbers are incorrect** across multiple employees

These issues need professional attention — and with 11 days remaining, the window is closing fast. Do not wait until March 28 to call your accountant.

Here is a realistic timeline for completing reconciliation with 11 days remaining:

| Days | Task |
|---|---|
| **Days 1–2 (March 20–21)** | Build master reconciliation spreadsheet; gather all payroll records, filed returns, and bank statements |
| **Days 3–4 (March 22–23)** | Calculate variances for all 12 months across all deduction types |
| **Days 5–6 (March 24–25)** | Investigate and resolve discrepancies; file any amended returns |
| **Days 7–8 (March 26–27)** | Employee-level spot checks; correct individual records |
| **Days 9–10 (March 28–29)** | Final review; prepare and submit annual returns (NHT S02) |
| **Day 11 (March 30)** | Buffer day for last-minute corrections |

Notice: March 31 is not on this timeline. You should not be doing reconciliation work on the deadline day. Everything should be filed by March 30 at the latest.
