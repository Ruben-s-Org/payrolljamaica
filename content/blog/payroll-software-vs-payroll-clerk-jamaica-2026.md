Every growing Jamaican business eventually faces the same question: **hire someone to handle payroll, or use software?**

## The Real Cost of a Payroll Clerk in Jamaica

### Salary

In Kingston, an experienced payroll clerk commands:
- **Entry-level (1–2 years experience):** J$800,000–J$1,000,000/year gross
- **Mid-level (3–5 years):** J$1,200,000–J$1,600,000/year gross
- **Senior (5+ years, CPA preferred):** J$1,800,000–J$2,400,000/year gross

In Montego Bay and Portmore, salaries run 10–15% lower.

### Employer Costs on Top of Salary

Remember: you pay statutory contributions ON TOP of their salary.

| Cost | Rate | Example (J$1,200,000 salary) |
|------|------|-------------------------------|
| Employer NIS | 3% | J$36,000/year |
| Employer NHT | 3% | J$36,000/year |
| Employer Education Tax | 3.5% | J$42,000/year |
| HEART Levy | 3% | J$36,000/year |
| **Total employer statutory cost** | **12.5%** | **J$150,000/year** |

**Total cost of a mid-level payroll clerk: J$1,350,000–J$1,750,000/year** — before benefits, office space, or equipment.

### Hidden Costs

- **Sick leave:** 10 days/year (Employment (Amendment) Act). Your payroll still has to run.
- **Annual leave:** 2+ weeks/year. Payroll doesn't stop.
- **Training:** TAJ regulations change. Someone has to keep up with them.
- **Recruitment:** If they leave, you're looking at J$150,000–J$300,000 in recruitment and onboarding costs.
- **Redundancy:** If you ever need to let them go, Employment Termination & Redundancy Payments Act calculations apply.

**True annual cost of an in-house payroll clerk: J$1,500,000–J$2,000,000+/year**

## Where Human Expertise Still Matters

Payroll software handles calculations perfectly. But there are situations where human HR/accounting expertise adds value:

**Genuine complexity cases:**
- Complex severance calculations for long-tenured staff
- Collective bargaining agreement (CBA) interpretation
- Disputed employee terminations
- TAJ audit defence
- Complex expatriate payroll (foreign tax treaties)

**For these situations:** You don't need a dedicated in-house payroll clerk. You need an accountant or HR consultant — on retainer or per-project. That's far cheaper than a full-time employee.

## The Compliance Risk You're Taking With a Human

### TAJ Rate Changes

Jamaica's statutory rates change. The PAYE threshold for 2026 is **J$1,500,096/year** — but many businesses are still using old thresholds from previous years.

If your payroll clerk doesn't update their formulas promptly:
- Employees get under-deducted → they owe TAJ at year-end
- Or employees get over-deducted → you owe them refunds
- Either way, your S01 Annual Return will have errors

**PayrollJamaica updates rates automatically** the moment they change. No action required by you.

### The Key-Person Risk

What happens when your payroll clerk:
- Goes on maternity leave?
- Gets sick the week payroll is due?
- Resigns with 2 weeks notice?

Payroll is a **hard deadline business.** Employees expect to be paid on time, every time. TAJ expects remittances by the 14th of every month, every month.

With software, you're never dependent on one person's availability.

### Calculation Errors and TAJ Audits

In a TAJ payroll audit, every error is your responsibility — not your clerk's. Common errors that trigger audits:

- Wrong annualisation of PAYE for part-time staff
- Missing employees on S01 return
- Incorrect NHT rates (employer 3%, employee 2% — often confused)
- Service charge distributions not treated as taxable wages

**Software doesn't forget. Humans do.**

## When a Payroll Clerk Makes Sense

To be fair, there are scenarios where in-house payroll staff make sense:

1. **Very large businesses (200+ employees)** with complex multi-department payroll structures
2. **Businesses with significant HR workload** beyond payroll (recruitment, performance management, grievances) — a combined HR/payroll role might justify the cost
3. **Businesses where the owner has zero time** to log into software and wants hands-off delegation
4. **Companies with complex union agreements** requiring constant interpretation

Even in these cases, the best approach is often: **software + a part-time HR generalist who manages exceptions**, rather than a dedicated payroll-only clerk.
